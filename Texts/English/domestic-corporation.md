# Domestic Corporation
## What Is a Domestic Corporation?

A domestic corporation is a company that conducts its affairs in its nation of origin. A domestic business is frequently taxed uniquely in contrast to a non-domestic business and might be required to pay duties or fees on the products it [imports](/import). Regularly, a domestic corporation can undoubtedly conduct business in different states or parts of the country where it has filed its [articles of incorporation](/articlesofincorporation).

Businesses that are situated in a country not the same as the one where they originated are alluded to as foreign corporations. Companies additionally might be alluded to as foreign businesses when they are outside of the state in which they were shaped. For instance, a corporation that is incorporated in [Delaware](/delaware-corporation) will be viewed as a domestic business there and a foreign business in any remaining states.

## Grasping Domestic Corporations

Generally, a [corporation](/corporation) is laid out after a business files its articles of incorporation with a state agency. From there on out, the corporation's all's conduct is subject to the law of the state in which it was shaped, even on the off chance that it isn't carrying on with work there. This likewise means that on the off chance that the company was incorporated under Nevada law, it will be viewed as a domestic corporation in that state and a foreign one wherever else.

Corporations are permitted to change which state laws oversee them. To turn into a domestic business in another state, the corporation must initially be [dissolved](/windingup) in the place where it was initially shaped. After that cycle is complete, the company might file the suitable articles of incorporation in another state.

Domestic business owners are free to pick where to domesticate their corporations and, accordingly, will look to examine corporate laws in various states to figure out which state addresses the most suitable home. By and large, Delaware has frequently been the preferred option.

> North of 66% of Fortune 500 companies are incorporated in the state of Delaware.
>

Delaware is perceived as a business-accommodating state and is especially known for its Court of Chancery. This unique court system is adept in settling complex corporate legal issues, including debates among shareholders. Delaware likewise has business-accommodating [usury laws](/usury-laws), giving banks and credit card companies more freedom to charge steep interest rates on loans.

## Special Considerations

For a domestic business that is choosing where to be incorporated, gauging which states have lower [corporate tax](/corporatetax) rates is certainly not a big consideration. Under federal tax laws, corporations are subject to taxation rates in the place where they carry on with work, not where they were shaped.
