# Form 706-NA
## What Is Form 706-NA: United States Estate (and Generation-Skipping Transfer) Tax Return?

Form 706-NA: United States Estate (and Generation-Skipping Transfer) Tax Return is a tax form distributed by the IRS that is utilized to compute [tax liabilities](/taxliability) for estates of people with U.S.- based assets that are part of their estate and who are not citizens and are not viewed as resident aliens.

## Who Can File Form 706-NA: United States Estate (and Generation-Skipping Transfer) Tax Return?

This IRS form is utilized to figure out the generation-skipping transfer tax liability for estates of non-resident aliens of the United States. The generation-skipping tax is demanded when the transfer of the decedent's estate happens instead of when it is received by a beneficiary.

A non-resident alien is somebody who doesn't live in the United States and isn't a citizen. The IRS has specific rules for how a person meets the criteria of a resident alien. Those non-citizens who don't fulfill these requirements would be viewed as non-resident aliens.

U.S.- based assets that would be viewed as part of an estate would incorporate things like real estate, physical personal property, and securities connected with U.S. companies. These American-based stocks would be subject to U.S. [estate taxes](/estatetax) even on the off chance that the certificates were physically stored outside of the country.

> Form 706-NA: United States Estate (and Generation-Skipping Transfer) Tax Return must be filed in no less than nine months following the person's death; be that as it may, a six-month cutoff time extension can be mentioned, which would permit the estate a total of 15 months to present their tax return desk work.
