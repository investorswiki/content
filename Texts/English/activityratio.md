# Activity Ratios
## What Is an Activity Ratio?

An activity ratio is a type of financial metric that demonstrates how proficiently a company is utilizing the assets on its balance sheet, to produce revenues and cash. Usually alluded to as [efficiency ratios](/efficiencyratio), activity ratios assist analysts with checking how a company handles [inventory management](/inventory-management), which is key to its operational smoothness and overall fiscal wellbeing.

## Figuring out Activity Ratios

Activity ratios are most helpful when employed to compare two contending businesses inside a similar industry to decide how a specific company stacks up among its friends. However, activity ratios may likewise be utilized to follow a company's fiscal progress over numerous recording periods, to recognize changes over the long haul. These numbers can be planned to introduce a forward-looking image of a company's prospective performance.

Activity ratios can be broken down into the following sub-classes:

### Accounts Receivable Turnover Ratio

The [accounts receivable turnover ratio](/receivableturnoverratio) decides an entity's ability to collect money from its customers. Total credit sales are separated by the average accounts receivable balance for a specific period. A low ratio recommends a deficiency in the collection cycle.

### Merchandise Inventory Turnover Ratio

The merchandise inventory turnover ratio measures how frequently the inventory balance is sold during a [accounting period](/accountingperiod). The [cost of goods sold](/pinions) is partitioned by the [average inventory](/average-inventory) for a specific period. Higher estimations propose that a company can move its inventory effortlessly.

### Total Assets Turnover Ratio

The total assets turnover ratio measures how productively an entity utilizes its assets to tender a sale. Total sales are partitioned by total assets to translate how capably a business utilizes its assets. More modest ratios might demonstrate that a company is battling to move its products.

### Return on Equity

A performance metric knows as return on equity (ROE) measures the revenues raised from shareholder equity. ROE is calculated by separating net income by all outstanding stock shares in the market.

### Asset Turnover Ratio

A measurement called the [asset turnover ratio](/assetturnover) measures the amount of revenue a company produces for each dollar of assets. This figure, which is just calculated by separating a company's sales by its total assets, uncovers how productively a company is utilizing its assets to create sales.

## Activity Ratios Vs. Profitability Ratios

Activity ratios and [profitability ratios](/profitabilityratios) are both fundamental insightful instruments that assist investors with assessing various features of a company's fiscal strength. Profitability ratios portray a company's profit generation, while productivity ratios measure how well a company uses its resources to create those profits. Profitability ratios might assist analysts with contrasting a company's profits and those of its industry rivals, while likewise tracking similar company's progress across several different reporting periods.

## Features
- An activity ratio comprehensively depicts any type of financial metric that assists investors and research analysts with measuring how effectively a company utilizes its assets to create revenues and cash.
- Activity ratios can be partitioned into merchandise inventory turnover ratios, total assets turnover ratios, return on equity measurements, and a range of different metrics.
- Activity ratios might be used to compare two distinct businesses inside a similar sector, or they might be utilized to monitor a single company's fiscal wellbeing over the long haul.
