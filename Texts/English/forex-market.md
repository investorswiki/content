# Forex Market
## What Is the Forex Market?

The forex market allows participants, like banks and individuals, to buy, sell or exchange currencies for both [hedging](/hedge) and speculative purposes. The [foreign exchange](/foreign-exchange-markets) (forex) market is the largest financial market in the world and is comprised of banks, commercial companies, central banks, investment management firms, hedge funds, retail [forex brokers](/currency-trading-forex-brokers), and investors.

## Understanding the Forex Market

The forex market isn't overwhelmed by a single market exchange, yet a global network of PCs and brokers from around the world. Forex brokers act as [market makers](/marketmaker) too and may post bid and ask prices for a [currency pair](/currencypair) that contrasts from the most competitive bid in the market.

The forex market is comprised of two levels — the [interbank market](/interbankmarket) and the [over-the-counter](/otc) (OTC) market. The interbank market is where large banks trade currencies for purposes, for example, hedging, balance sheet adjustments, and for clients. The OTC market, then again, is where individuals trade through online platforms and brokers.

From Monday morning in Asia to Friday evening in New York, the forex market is a 24-hour market, meaning it doesn't close overnight. The forex market opens from Sunday at 5 p.m. EST to Friday at 4 p.m. EST.

This varies from markets like equities, bonds, and commodities, which all close for a while, generally in the late evening EST. Notwithstanding, likewise with most things, there are special cases. Some [emerging market](/emergingmarketeconomy) currencies close for a while during the trading day.

## History of the Forex Market

Up until World War I, [currencies](/currency) were pegged to precious metals, like gold and silver. Then, after the Second World War, the system fell and was supplanted by the [Bretton Woods agreement](/brettonwoodsagreement). That agreement brought about the creation of three international organizations to work with economic activity across the globe. They were the following:

1. [International Monetary Fund](/imf) (IMF)
1. [General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade](/gatt) (GATT)
1. [International Bank for Reconstruction and Development](/international-bank-of-reconstruction-and-development) (IBRD)

The new system likewise supplanted gold with the U.S. dollar as a peg for international currencies. The U.S. government vowed to back up dollar supplies with equivalent gold reserves. Be that as it may, the Bretton Woods system became excess in 1971 when U.S. President Richard Nixon announced a "impermanent" suspension of the dollar's convertibility into gold.

Currencies are presently free to pick their own [peg](/currency-peg) and their not set in stone by supply and demand in international markets.

## Type of Forex Markets

Three are three key types of forex markets: spot, forward, and futures.

### Spot Forex Market

The spot market is the immediate exchange of currency among buyers and sellers at the current exchange rate. The spot market makes up a significant part of the currency trading.

The key participants in the spot market incorporate commercial, investment, and central banks, as well as dealers, brokers, and examiners. Large commercial and investment banks make up a major portion of spot trades, trading for themselves as well as for their customers.

### Forward Forex Market

In the [forward markets](/forward-exchange-contract), two parties consent to trade a currency at a set cost and quantity sometime not too far off. No currency is exchanged when the trade is initiated. The two parties can be companies, individuals, governments, or something like that. Forward markets are helpful for hedging.

On the downside, forward markets lack centralized trading and are somewhat illiquid (since there are just the two parties). Too, there is counterparty risk, which is that the other part will default.

### Futures Forex Market

Future markets are like forward markets in terms of fundamental function. Notwithstanding, the big difference is that future markets utilize centralized exchanges. Because of centralized exchanges, there are no counterparty risks for either party. This guarantees future markets are highly liquid, particularly compared to forward markets.

### Benefits

One of the biggest benefits of forex trading is the lack of limitations and inherent flexibility. There's an extremely large amount of trading volume and markets are open very nearly 24/7. With that, individuals who work everyday positions can likewise partake in trading at night or on the ends of the week (in contrast to the stock market).

There's a large amount of flexibility with regards to accessible trading options. There are many currency pairs, and there are different types of agreements, like a future or spot agreement. The costs for transactions are generally extremely low versus other markets and the allowed leverage is among the highest of every financial market, which can amplify gains (as well as losses).

### Inconveniences

With forex markets, there are leverage risks — the very leverage that offers benefits. Forex trading allows for large amounts of leverage. The leverage allowed is 20-30 times and can offer outsized returns, however can likewise mean large losses rapidly.

Albeit the fact that it operates almost 24 hours daily can be a positive for some, it likewise means that a few traders should utilize calculations or trading programs to safeguard their investments while they are away. This adds to operational risks and can increase costs.

The other major hindrance is [counterparty risk,](/counterpartyrisk) where directing Forex markets can be troublesome, given an international market trades continually. There is no central exchange that guarantees a trade, and that means there could be default risk.
