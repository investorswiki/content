# American Academy of Financial Management (AAFM)
## What Was the American Academy of Financial Management (AAFM)?

The American Academy of Financial Management (AAFM) was an organization with individuals crossing in excess of 151 countries across the globe that offered exclusive assignments for financial professionals. The people who earned certifications as well as sanctions from the AAFM were recognized as meeting probably the highest standards in the industry from the International Board of Standards (IBS). AAFM failed to exist in 2015 and was trailed by the Global Academy of Finance and Management (GAFM).

## Grasping the American Academy of Financial Management (AAFM)

The American Academy of Financial Management (AAFM) offered certifications and sanctions for those in industry jobs like a financial professional, wealth manager, market analyst, financial and investment planner, asset manager, trust and estate planning analyst, or an economist.

AAFM offered several industries with recognized graduate assignments to qualified professionals. The organization likewise filled a void for nearly 120,000 financial professionals working as [MBAs](/mba), CPAs, lawyers, and PhDs.

Established in 1996, it was first settled as a professional organization for [investment managers](/investment-manager), lawyers, and analysts, when the American Academy of Financial Management and Analysts merged with the Founders Advisory Committee of the Original Tax and Estate Planning Law Review.

AAFM quit existing in 2015 and was trailed by the Global Academy of Finance and Management (GAFM), to which it sold its intellectual property and its logo, which GAFM utilizes. GAFM offers a considerable lot of the very certificates that AAFM offered.

## Who the American Academy of Financial Management (AAFM) Served

AAFM served professionals in the United States as well as Hong Kong, Beijing, India, Dubai, Kuwait, the Americas, Singapore, the Caribbean, Europe, and that's just the beginning. Training partners included Deutsche Bank, Citibank, Xerox, NASA, HSBC Bank, China Construction Bank, National Bank of Kuwait, the Government of Dubai, BAE Systems, the United States [Securities and Exchange Commission](/sec) (SEC), the U.S. Naval force, the Department of Energy, the Department of Interior, 3M Asia Pacific, Dow Chemical, Hewlett Packard Singapore, Indian Overseas Bank, Shangri La Hotels, and in a real sense hundreds more.

Albeit the Academy kept up with severe intellectual and experiential standards for its understudies, its certifications required less thorough study and were less widely acknowledged/regarded than the [CFA](/cfa) or [CFP](/cfp).
