# Peer Group
## What Is a Peer Group?

The term peer group refers to a group of individuals or companies that share comparable characteristics with one another. These characteristics might be age, education, ethnic background, size, [industry](/industry), or sector. Peer groups are known for their influential nature as they are able to shape the decisions of members of the group. In that capacity, peer groups often contain hierarchies, with clear leaders who sit at the top. Peer groups are often used in analysis in a number of academic and professional fields.

## Understanding Peer Groups

As noted above, peer groups contain a number of people or other entities that share one or more comparable characteristics. Peer groups permit individuals to be grouped together by certain defining features, like age, [income](/income), education, race, and additionally gender. Qualities among members for corporate peer groups include size, industry, [sector](/sector), or potentially financial position.

Peer groups are used in a number of different fields, for example, [finance](/finance) and [marketing](/marketing), as well as humanism. They are known for the influence they have among group members. They comprise of hierarchies, which place one or a few individual group members at the top. These members can often shape the activities and decisions of the other group members.

Peer group analysis or peer comparison involves an apples-to-apples comparison. This means the constituents of the peer group ought to be more or less like one another. Comparisons permit professionals to identify trends and anomalies in behavior and outcomes, and give them an opportunity to figure out [efficiencies](/efficiency) and opportunities:

While perhaps not immediately self-evident, peer groups are sometimes identified by a given company in its [10-K](/10-k) filing and quite often in its [proxy filing](/proxystatement), however the latter can be more expansive in terms of specific business sectors and is used to set executive compensation plans.

> Insurance companies compile peer groups to underwrite life or health insurance policies on certain demographics, for example, age-related groups or those who do or don't smoke.

## Utilizing Peer Groups

[Peer comparison](/peer_perform) is one of the most widely used and accepted methods of equity analysis used by professional analysts, individual investors, and professionals.

### Investment Research

Because companies in a peer group share comparable traits, they lend themselves to relative value analysis. Peer group analysis establishes a [valuation](/valuation) for stocks in investment research, as long as the group comprises of companies like the one being researched. This is especially key when it comes to business areas and [market capitalization](/marketcapitalization). Investors can use this analysis to spot valuation [anomalies](/abnormality) for certain stocks.

For example, a stock trading at a [earnings multiple](/earningsmultiplier) of 15x (compared with a 10x multiple for its peer group) might be considered [overvalued](/overvalued). Investors can uncover reasons for the higher earnings multiple and ultimately determine that it is deserved.

Relative valuation among peers in a group can be efficient and effective, quickly showing which stocks might be overvalued, and which could make great augmentations to a portfolio. While there are other methods to determine when a stock is worth buying, for example, discounted [cash flow](/cashflow) or [technical analysis](/technicalanalysis), peer comparison analysis remains a key tool for uncovering [undervalued](/undervalued) stocks.

Because the data necessary to conduct the analysis is generally public and readily accessible on financial websites, it is easy for anyone to begin employing this method of analysis to identify opportunities.

> Peer group comparisons are likewise called comparables or comps.
>
### Marketing

Peer groups are important in advertising and marketing, especially in the age of [social media](/social-media). Leaders in the group or those who are at the top of the hierarchical ladder are commonly known as influencers. Professionals study peer groups to show how they influence buying patterns and consumer [trends](/trend) for advertising and product development purposes.

## Advantages and Disadvantages of Peer Groups

Peer group analysis helps shape numerous decisions in the financial and investment universes. There are a number of different benefits to utilizing peer groups, especially in investment analysis. However, just like whatever other logical tool, there are drawbacks to this form of analysis also. We've listed some of the most common ones below.

### Advantages

One of the clearest benefits of utilizing peer group analysis is that it helps investors and analysts uncover undervalued stocks. This is often accomplished by dissecting different metrics, including [leverage](/leverage) and profitability among others. Doing so permits the individual to determine companies that ought to carry higher-than-average valuations. A stock might be considered a buying opportunity in the event that the current valuation is lower than what seems reasonable.

Investors and professionals don't have to go far to get the information they need to perform peer group analysis. Data is easily available, either on company websites or through filings done with the [Securities and Exchange Commission](/sec) (SEC). This means the individual doesn't need to do exhaustive research.

Conducting peer comparisons give investors a chance to determine certain anomalies and trends that arise with different companies and in the market in general.

### Disadvantages

Doing a peer comparison isn't so easy as it seems. That is because quantitative factors aren't the main considerations. Truth be told, there are qualitative issues that must be taken into account. This means that the analysis can be genuinely subjective.

Peer groups often come with certain biases, which can influence an analyst or investor's analysis. For example, some groups come with [survivorship bias](/survivorshipbias). This means that the peer group may not contain any companies that underperform. This type of analysis, therefore, doesn't take companies that have gone under or phased out of the group into account.

There may not be very many companies in the peer group. When this is the case, peer comparison becomes ineffective. For it to work, there needs to get a sizeable group of companies that can measure up to one another.

## Example of a Peer Group

Peer groups refer to companies that are in the same industry or sector. These are competitors that are generally the same size. Peer groups can be found in analyst research reports or an individual company's financial statements.

Lockheed Martin is an aerospace, defense, and security company headquartered in Maryland. As per the company's 2017 proxy statement, its peer group comprises of other comparable companies as its peers, including General Dynamics, Raytheon, and Northrup Grumman. ## Peer Group FAQs
### What Are the Types of Peer Groups?

There are several different types of peer groups, including social groups (cliques, sports teams, etc). In investing and finance, peers might be grouped by industry or sector (consumer staples, retail, telecoms), size (generally by market cap), financial health (profitability, capital structure), or business factors (business models, location, seasonality).

### What Is Peer Group Analysis?

Peer group analysis is a process including the comparison of entities that share comparative characteristics. It is an apples-to-apples comparison of subjects that are genuinely like one another. This kind of analysis considers the identification of trends and anomalies in behavior and outcomes.

### What Is a Peer Group Index?

A peer group index is one that groups individual companies together into a single, benchmark index. For instance, the [S&P 500](/sp500) can be considered a peer group index. This index is comprised of 500 of the largest public companies in the United States and is generally considered the best gauge of U.S. large-cap equities.

### How Might You Use a Peer Group Average in Investing?

A peer group average is considered one of the best and efficient methods for determining the valuation of a security. Put simply, investors can use certain metrics drawn from corporate financial statements to assess a certain company's stock price compared to its peers.

### Why Are Firms Classified Into Peer Groups for Ratio Analysis?

Companies are grouped into peer groups for ratio analysis as part of fundamental analysis. It permits researchers to determine individual company liquidity, efficiencies, as well as profitability. This is done by examining each company's past and current financial statements and comparing them with one another. Some of the most popular ratios include [price-to-earnings (P/E)](/price-earningsratio), [price-to-book](/price-to-bookratio), and price-to-dividend ratios.

## The Bottom Line

Peer groups comprise of comparative entities that share one or more characteristics in common. They serve a very important purpose in a wide range of fields of study. They can be one of the most useful tools when it comes to equity analysis for investors and financial professionals. Anyone can perform peer group comparisons utilizing information that is widely available on corporate websites or through the SEC.
