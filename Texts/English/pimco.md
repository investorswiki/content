# PIMCO (Pacific Investment Management Co.)
## What Is PIMCO (Pacific Investment Management Co.)?

PIMCO, or the Pacific Investment Management Company, is an American [investment management](/investment-management) firm established in 1971 in California. The firm focuses on fixed income and oversees more than $2.2 trillion in [assets](/asset). The firm fundamentally handles portfolio management, account management, and business management.

PIMCO has some expertise in [fixed-income](/fixedincome) securities. It deals with the universally realized Total Return Fund. The company serves [institutional investors](/institutionalinvestor), high-net-worth individuals, and individual investors with its account services and mutual funds.

## Grasping PIMCO

PIMCO was established in 1971 in Newport Beach, California, by Bill Gross, Jim Muzzy, and Bill Podlich. The firm sent off with a total of $12 million in assets and the conviction that [bonds](/bond) ought to be actively traded to improve [returns](/return).

The firm has since expanded to derivatives, mortgage-based securities, emerging markets, and different sectors of the global fixed income market. It has become one of the biggest asset management firms in the world. When a unit of Pacific Mutual Life Insurance, it is presently owned by German financial services firm Allianz SE.

Starting around 2014, Gross had left the firm for Janus Capital Group, Inc. Gross' likely successor, Mohamed El-Erian, likewise left. In April 2015, former Federal Reserve Chair [Ben Bernanke](/benbernanke) was recruited as a senior advisor to PIMCO.

## PIMCO's Strategies

PIMCO's investment interaction integrates bits of knowledge from its Cyclical Forums, which expect market and economic trends north of a 6-to year period, and the annual Secular Forum, which undertakings trends more than a 3-to 5-year period. The company says it accepts that an educated [macroeconomic](/macroeconomics) viewpoint — over lengthy and short-term skylines — is key to recognizing opportunities and likely risks.

## PIMCO's Total Return Fund

The company's Total Return Fund seeks to boost capital while preserving [capital](/capital). The fund, laid out in 1987, underscores higher-quality, intermediate-term bonds and is all the more globally diversified, in this way expecting to reduce concentration risk. The fund additionally has flexibility that answers changing economic conditions.

The fund pays a month to month [dividend](/dividend) and covers the U.S. investment-grade, fixed-rate bond market with index components for government and corporate securities, mortgage pass-through securities, and asset-backed securities.

## PIMCO Today

According to the company's website, PIMCO has in excess of 3,050 employees who work in offices throughout the Americas, Europe, and Asia. The company flaunts in excess of 900 global investment experts and more than 260 [portfolio managers](/portfoliomanager). As of June 2021, the company managed more than $2.20 trillion in assets.

The company partners with various institutions, from corporations, [central banks](/centralbank), private and public pension funds, [endowments](/enrichment), and establishments, as well as retirement plans.
