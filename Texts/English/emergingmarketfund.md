# Emerging Market Fund
## What Is an Emerging Market Fund?

An emerging market fund alludes to a fund that invests the majority of its assets in securities from countries with economies that are viewed as emerging. Funds that specialize in emerging markets range from [mutual funds](/mutualfund) to exchange-traded funds (ETFs). These countries are in an emerging growth phase and offer high possible returns with higher risks than [developed market countries](/developed-economy).

Emerging market funds normally specialize in either fixed-income or equity securities from these regions.

## How an Emerging Market Fund Works

The [infrastructures](/infrastructure) and economies of emerging market nations shift broadly across the world. These countries are in high-growth phases with quickly expanding and further developing market conditions. Factors impacting their classification incorporate macroeconomic conditions, quickly expanding [gross domestic product](/gdp) (GDP) rates, political stability, capital market processes, and financial market trading and settlement procedures. Many emerging market economies likewise experience huge growth from middle-class consumers who help to drive increased demand across business sectors.

An emerging market fund is a type of mutual fund or [ETF](/etf), which invests vigorously in securities of changing [asset classes](/assetclasses) — stocks, bonds, and different securities — from creating or emerging market economies. The most common spots where these funds invest in incorporate India, China, Russia, and Brazil. Below, you'll track down a more exhaustive rundown of emerging market nations.

Emerging market funds try to capitalize on the return opportunity introduced by emerging market economies. Funds might invest in emerging market debt or equity to build a diversified fund offering for investors. Various debt and equity options are accessible for investors seeking to invest in a single country or a diversified portfolio of [emerging market countries](/emergingmarketeconomy).

In the emerging markets category, investors will likewise view as both passive and [active funds](/activemanagement) giving emerging market exposure across the market segment. Funds offer a scope of options across the risk range — cash risk, inflation risk, political risk, and liquidity risk, among others — and are generally attractive investments for growth investors.

## Special Considerations

Companies are normally sorted in view of where their economies are in terms of improvement — developed, frontier, or emerging. [Developed nations](/developed-economy), likewise alluded to as industrial nations, have completely developed economies with infrastructure that is mechanically advanced. [Frontier economies](/frontier-market) are somewhat less developed than a completely industrialized nation, however somewhat more than an emerging economy.

Then, at that point, there are the emerging market economies. As indicated over, these countries offer higher returns with higher [risk](/risk), relative to developed market countries. They are normally viewed as more stable than frontier markets. Emerging market countries can be recognized by market index suppliers and are defined by different qualities.

The emerging markets likewise offer market segments that are attractive for investment. [Asia ex-Japan](/asiaexjapan) emerging market funds incorporate securities from Asia excluding Japan. This region offers exposure to the Asian emerging markets. Also, [BRIC](/bric) funds will incorporate securities from Brazil, Russia, India, and China. The BRIC countries are known to be four of the most predominant economics in the emerging markets.

## Types of Emerging Market Fund Securities

Here is a glance at probably the most common types of securities that make up emerging market funds on the market.

### Emerging Market Debt

Emerging market debt can offer the least risk among emerging market investments. [Credit](/credit) quality is a leading objective that separates emerging market [debt funds](/debtfund), furnishing access to debt investments with changing levels of risk. Investors can invest in both passive and active funds. Leading indexes for passive market investment incorporate the J.P. Morgan Emerging Markets Bond Index and the Bloomberg Emerging Markets USD Aggregate Index.

### Emerging Market Equity

Emerging market equity includes a broad scope of companies from emerging markets around the world. Investors can invest in passive indexes for emerging markets exposure or look for actively managed funds. Top indexes incorporate the [MSCI Emerging Markets Index](/emergingmarketsindex) and the S&P Global Broad Market Index.

## Examples of Emerging Market Funds

The American Funds Emerging Markets Bond Fund (EBNAX) is a wise investment for investors seeking exposure to emerging market debt. This fund is actively managed and invests in emerging market government and [corporate bonds](/corporatebond). Starting around 2022, its biggest holdings were in Mexico, Russia, and China.

The American Century Emerging Markets Fund (TWMIX) is an example of an emerging markets [equity fund](/equityfund). The Fund is actively managed and involves fundamental analysis to pick stock investments for the portfolio. As of Q1 2022, the fund's top countries were in three emerging market countries including China at 43%, Taiwan at 14%, and South Korea at 13%.
