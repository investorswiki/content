# Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) Criteria
## What Are Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) Criteria?

Environmental, social, and governance (ESG) criteria are a set of standards for a company's behavior utilized by socially conscious investors to screen likely investments. Environmental criteria look at how as a company defends the environment, including corporate policies tending to climate change, for instance. Social criteria look at how it oversees associations with employees, providers, customers, and the networks where it operates. Governance manages a company's leadership, executive pay, [audits](/review), [internal controls](/internalcontrols), and shareholder rights.

## How Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) Criteria Work

Investors have, in recent years, shown interest in putting their money where their values are.

Accordingly, brokerage firms and mutual fund companies have begun offering [exchange-traded funds (ETFs)](/etf) and other financial products that follow ESG criteria. [Robo-advisors](/roboadvisor-roboadviser) including Betterment and Wealthfront have advanced these ESG-themed offerings to more youthful investors.

ESG criteria are additionally progressively educating the investment decisions regarding large institutional investors, for example, public pension funds. As per the latest report from US SIF Foundation, investors held $17.1 trillion in assets picked by ESG criteria toward the finish of 2019, up from $12 trillion just two years sooner.

ESG investing is now and then alluded to as [sustainable](/sustainability) investing, responsible investing, impact investing, or [socially responsible investing (SRI)](/sri). To survey a company in view of ESG criteria, investors check out at a broad scope of behaviors and policies.

## Types of Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) Criteria

ESG investors try to guarantee the companies they fund are responsible stewards of the environment, great corporate residents and are driven by accountable managers.

### Environmental

Environmental criteria might incorporate corporate climate policies, energy use, squander, pollution, natural resource protection, and treatment of animals. The criteria can likewise assist with assessing any environmental risks a company could face and how the company is dealing with those risks.

Contemplations might incorporate direct and indirect ozone depleting substance emissions, management of toxic waste, and compliance with environmental regulations.

### Social

Social criteria take a gander at the company's associations with [stakeholders](/stakeholder).

Does it hold providers to its own ESG standards? Does the company give a percentage of its profits to the nearby community or encourage employees to perform charitable effort there?

Do working environment conditions reflect high respect for employees' wellbeing and safety? Or on the other hand does the company exploit its customers?

### Governance

ESG governance standards guarantee a company utilizes accurate and transparent accounting methods, seeks after integrity and diversity in choosing its leadership, and is accountable to shareholders.

ESG investors might require confirmations that companies keep away from [conflicts of interest](/irreconcilable circumstance) in their decision of board individuals and senior executives, don't utilize political contributions to acquire particular treatment, or participate in unlawful conduct.

## Special Considerations

Investment firms following ESG criteria frequently set their own needs. For instance, Boston-based Trillium Asset Management, with $5.6 billion under management as of December 2021, utilizes an assortment of ESG factors to assist with distinguishing companies situated for strong long-term performance.

The criteria are set by analysts who distinguish the significant issues facing specific sectors, industries, and companies. Trillium's ESG criteria block investments in the following:

- Companies that operate in higher-risk areas or have exposure to coal or hard rock mining, nuclear or coal power, private penitentiaries, agricultural biotechnology, tobacco, tar sands, or weapons and guns.
- Companies associated with major or recent contentions over human rights, animal welfare, environmental worries, governance issues, or product safety.

Interestingly, Trillium searches for investments meeting the following ESG criteria:

### Environment
- Distributes a carbon or sustainability report
- Limits hurtful toxins and synthetic compounds
- Tries to bring down ozone depleting substance emissions
- Utilizes renewable energy sources

### Social
- Operates an ethical supply chain
- Upholds LGBTQ rights and encourages diversity
- Has policies to safeguard against sexual misconduct
- Pays fair wages

### Governance
- Embraces diversity on board of directors
- Embraces corporate transparency
- Somebody other than the CEO is chair of the board

## Advantages and disadvantages of Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) Criteria

In years past, the socially responsible investor was assumed to forfeit self-interest somewhat by staying away from certain investments in view of non-financial criteria. All things considered, tobacco and defense, two industries kept away from by numerous ESG investors, have generally delivered well-better than expected market returns.

All the more recently, some have contended that, notwithstanding their social value, ESG criteria can assist investors with staying away from the blowups that happen while companies operating in an unsafe or unethical way are ultimately held accountable for its ramifications. Models incorporate BP's (BP) 2010 Gulf of Mexico oil spill and Volkswagen's emissions scandal, which shook the companies' stock prices and cost them billions of dollars.

As ESG-disapproved of business practices get some decent forward movement, investment firms are progressively tracking their performance. Financial services companies like JPMorgan Chase (JPM), Wells Fargo (WFC), and Goldman Sachs (GS) have distributed annual reports that widely survey their ESG approaches and the main concern results.

The ultimate value of ESG criteria will rely upon whether they encourage companies to drive real change for a long term benefit, or simply check boxes and distribute reports. That, thusly, will rely upon whether the investment flows follow ESG criteria that are realistic, quantifiable, and actionable.
