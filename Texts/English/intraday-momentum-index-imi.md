# Intraday Momentum Index (IMI)
## What Is the Intraday Momentum Index (IMI)?

The Intraday Momentum Index (IMI), is a technical indicator that joins parts of [candlestick](/candlestick) analysis with the [relative strength index](/rsi) (RSI) to produce overbought or oversold signals.

The intraday indicator was developed by market technician Tushar Chande to aid investors with their trading choices.

## Understanding the Intraday Momentum Index (IMI)

Investors use [technical indicators](/technicalindicator) to estimate when a security, like a stock, ought to be bought or sold. Technical analysis, which utilizes technical indicators, inspects the relationship between a security's price and [volume](/volume) over differed periods of time. Indicators, for example, the relative strength index and [Bollinger bands](/bollingerbands), try to create buy and sell signals without inspecting a security's [fundamentals](/fundamentals). In that capacity, they are generally viewed as more valuable for short-term traders than long-term investors.

The IMI takes a gander at the relationship between a security's open and close price throughout the day, instead of how the open/close price fluctuates between days. It consolidates a few highlights of the relative strength index, specifically the relationship between "up closes" and "down closes" and whether there is an indication that a stock is overbought or oversold, with candlestick charts. Candlestick charts for a given day contain a "[real body](/realbody)" highlighting the gap between the open and close endlessly price points over the high and low called upper and lower [shadows](/shadow).

> Technical analysts can utilize the IMI to guess when a security is [overbought](/overbought) or [oversold](/oversold).

The IMI is calculated as the sum of gains on up days separated by the sum of gains on up days plus the sum of losses on down days. This is then duplicated by 100. In the event that the subsequent number is greater than 70, the security is considered overbought, while a perusing of under 30 demonstrates that a security is oversold. The investor will take a gander at the IMI over a period of days, with 14 days being the most common time period to check out.
