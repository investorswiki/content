# Vendor Note
## What Is a Vendor Note?

A vendor note is a short-term loan a [vendor](/vendor) makes to a customer that is secured by goods the customer purchases from the vendor. A vendor note is classified as a form of "vendor finance" or "[vendor financing](/vendorfinancing)," which is a type of lending that normally appears as a [deferred loan](/deferredinterest) made by a vendor. Vendor notes are probably going to be employed when a vendor has more confidence in a customer's business possibilities than a traditional lender (a bank) would.

## Understanding Vendor Notes

Vendor notes can be a helpful and convenient form of financing, especially when deep rooted vendors with different customer puts together are taking with respect to new, smaller buyers who commonly have small amounts of [working capital](/workingcapital) with which to purchase inventory or essential goods.

At times, customers might be completely dependent on vendor note financing to secure essential inventory or equipment. The utilization of such vendor financing can make it more straightforward for a company to increase sales volume and revenue, however in doing so it likewise incurs the risk of the buyers it finances not paying back their loans.

Vendor note loans are frequently secured by the [inventory](/inventory) being sold to the buyer however may likewise be backed by vows of the buyer's business assets or cash flow. The utilization of a vendor note will generally denote a decent relationship among vendor and customer.

## Terms of Vendor Notes

Vendor notes fluctuate in terms of their chance to maturity, yet notes with [time horizons](/timehorizon) in the scope of three to five years are considered common. A wide range of types of terms and conditions can be incorporated into a vendor note, for example, limitations on the types of business rehearses the buyer can participate in, restrictions on acquiring other inventory or business assets, and requirements that specific financial ratios or benchmarks be maintained.

While vendor notes will generally amount to deferred loans, in some cases there might be an interest charge on the borrowed sum (the value of goods that has changed hands). While vendors would surely really like to be paid promptly for goods or services they render, maintaining a relationship by helping with financing and being paid back over the long haul (in some cases with interest) is better than no sale by any means.

## Benefits and Disadvantages of Vendor Notes

Depending on the inventory or equipment you plan on buying, vendor notes, or vendor financing by and large, accompany a few benefits and weaknesses. It's important to ensure whether vendor financing or a traditional bank loan will be a better option for your business.

One of the primary benefits of a vendor note is purchasing equipment at a decent value. Numerous vendors have [in-house financing](/inhousefinancing) departments that offer customers discounts on the things they need for their business. As the product and financing are coming straightforwardly from the manufacturer, the deal you receive might be better than you'd find somewhere else, making it an alluring option.

Another strong selling point of a vendor note is the simplicity of obtaining the purchase and financing all the while. A manufacturer needs to make the sale and comprehends the product, gave the financing to them is direct. A bank, on the other hand, should assess the manufacturer, the product being sold, and the buyer, which is a significantly longer cycle.

> There are generally two types of vendor financing: debt financing, for example, a vendor note, and [equity financing](/equityfinancing), where the vendor receives stock or equity in the business.
>

Obtaining financing straightforwardly from the vendor likewise regularly accompanies lower upfront costs and it is simpler to refresh your equipment over the long run since you are dealing straightforwardly with the manufacturer.

One of the main hindrances of vendor notes is in the event that the vendor doesn't have an in-house financing department and you need to deal with a third-party non-bank financing entity. In the event that this entity is curious about the vendor or the equipment, it might charge a higher interest rate or finance only a small portion of the cost.

In addition, assuming you are purchasing utilized equipment, the costs may likewise be higher on the grounds that the manufacturer incentives never again apply, and the vendor will think about that while pricing the financing.

Ultimately, a few vendors will only give financing to the equipment however not include whatever other terms that might benefit your business, including transportation and training costs.

## Illustration of a Vendor Note

Another medical office buyer needs to obtain a laser gadget utilized for special short term medical procedures at a cost of $1,000,000. It has just $100,000 to spend. Instead of going to a lender to ask for a business loan, a medical gadget vendor will offer the piece of equipment to the customer under the agreement that the medical office buyer pays back the $900,000 balance of the medical gadget over a period of five years at an interest rate of 2%.

In that capacity, the vendor will carry a note until the $900,000 is repaid. The buyer gets the gadget, which will add a revenue stream, the seller gets a sale and pockets the interest on the loan. It might likewise get follow-on business from the buyer.

## The Bottom Line

A vendor note can be a decent option for a business seeking to obtain inventory or equipment. It is an especially decent option for another business that doesn't have huge levels of cash or [credit history](/credit-history) to obtain a traditional bank loan. Vendor notes likewise lay out a relationship between the vendor and a business that could be productive to both later on.

Choosing a vendor note or traditional bank loan will rely upon what is best for your business, so gauging the upsides and downsides of each before making a commitment is important.

## FAQ
### What Is Vendor Financing?
Vendor financing will be financing that a business obtains straightforwardly from the vendor while purchasing inventory or equipment. Vendor financing remains in contrast to traditional methods of financing, for example, obtaining a loan from a bank. Vendor financing is much of the time a deferred loan and can lead to building a strong relationship between a vendor and a customer.
### What Is a Vendor Loan Agreement?
A vendor loan agreement is a financing agreement between a vendor and a buyer where the vendor consents to give capital to the buyer to the purchase of the vendor's product. The buyer will pay an upfront cost, and the remainder of the cost, plus interest, will be paid after some time or sometime in the not too distant future later on. The vendor loan agreement spreads out each of the terms of the financing arrangement, for example, the gatherings to the agreement, the amount financed, the opportunity to maturity, and the interest rate.
### Are Vendor Notes Subordinated Debt?
Vendor notes are subordinated debt, meaning they sit below senior debt in the repayment waterfall in the event of bankruptcy. Holders of subordinated debt are constantly paid after holders of senior debt are paid off.
### What Happens If You Default on a Vendor Note?
The repercussions of defaulting on a vendor note can change depending on the arrangement and the terms of the vendor loan agreement. The vendor can repossess the inventory or equipment that was sold, as well as different assets of the business to recover its losses. The vendor may likewise be qualified for future cash flows of the business.
