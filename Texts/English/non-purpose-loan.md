# Non-Purpose Loan
## What Is a Non-Purpose Loan?

A non-purpose loan is an alternative type of loan that frequently includes involving investment securities as [collateral](/collateral) and depends on complex organizing. Regulated non-purpose loans can be offered by businesses and financial institutions with some specific government-administrative documentation requirements.

## How a Non-Purpose Loan Works

Regulated non-purpose loans permit a borrower to involve an investment portfolio as loan collateral, however the proceeds can't be utilized for purchasing, carrying or trading securities. One advantage of this type of loan is that it gives investors access to funds without selling their investments.

Generally, non-purpose loans may likewise be highlighted as a lending category across an assortment of lending platforms. Typically, lenders will require a borrower to determine a loan purpose for a personal loan. This is particularly important with online lending platforms where retail and institutional investors decide to invest in loans by their specific purpose.

Regulations require financial institutions to reveal whether a loan is a non-purpose or purpose loan. This is regulated by the Federal Reserve under Regulation U. Borrowers getting a non-purpose loan must complete a compliance form itemizing the terms of the loan and its non-purpose obligations.

### Loan Platform Categories

As a rule, online loan platforms may likewise offer non-purpose loans which are personal loans got by borrowers with no specific purpose for their utilization. Online lending investors in platforms like Lending Club or Prosper will frequently invest in platform loans in light of the loan's purpose so this classification can likewise give consideration in investment risk analysis.

## Non-Purpose Loan versus Margin Loan

Non-purpose loans are viewed as an alternative to traditional [margin](/marginaccount) borrowing since they permit various investment accounts to be utilized to secure a loan. Both non-purpose and margin loans will permit investors to keep on getting the benefits of their portfolio holdings, like dividends, interest and appreciation. Both are likewise subject to a [margin call](/margincall) in the event that the value of the pledged securities declines below the predefined limit. Nonetheless, there are differences between these two types of borrowing.

Non-purpose loans are typically promoted as securities backed lines of credit (SBLOCs). They are generally more complex to get than a standard margin loan. What's more, as verified above, they can't be utilized to buy securities though margin loans are typically utilized for the sole purpose of investing in securities.

Financiers offer margin loans on individual investment accounts. SBLOCs offer borrowers the opportunity to get a loan using various account investments. Some SBLOCs might require a specific account to get the lending proceeds.

## Illustration of a Non-Purpose Loan

Charles Schwab gives one model its Pledged Asset Line of Credit product. Generally, borrowers can access up to 70% of their collateral assets as cash through the loan agreement. Terms of as long as five years are accessible and the main applicable fees are late fees. Like all non-purpose loans, the Schwab's Pledged Asset Line can't be utilized to buy securities.
