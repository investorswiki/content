# PayPal
## What Is PayPal?

PayPal is a [electronic commerce](/ecommerce) company that works with payments between parties through online transfers. PayPal allows customers to lay out an account on its platform, which is associated with a client's [credit card](/creditcard) or checking account. When identification and proof of funds are confirmed, users can start sending or getting payments to and from other PayPal accounts online or through the company's app.

## How PayPal Works

PayPal offers payment services and answers for both personal consumers and businesses. The company allows personal consumers to shop, make payments, and transfer money no sweat. Users need an email address to pursue an account and must give a credit card, [debit card](/debitcard), or bank account to complete the setup. PayPal checks all the information to ensure the person setting up the [account](/account) is the actual owner before the service can be utilized.

PayPal account holders can utilize the company's website or mobile app to [transfer](/transfer) money to others utilizing the beneficiary's email address or mobile telephone number. Shoppers can pick the PayPal option to complete purchases online assuming the retailer has the service. [Transactions](/transaction) are completed in no time and the company guarantees that transfers are accessible for payment or withdrawal to a bank account right away.

PayPal likewise offers businesses a scope of answers for their [day-to-day operations](/working exercises). This incorporates payment entries for both online and in-person transactions, business management arrangements, as well as credit and [financing](/financing) options. Business owners must likewise give an email address to make an account.

PayPal endeavors to make online purchases more secure by giving a form of payment that doesn't need the payor or payee to uncover credit card or bank account numbers. In this manner, money is secure, privacy is protected, and, since the customer base is so large, transactions are quicker than traditional methods.

## Special Considerations

In spite of the fact that PayPal isn't a bank, it is as yet subject to a large number of similar consumer protection regulations by which banks are represented. For instance, the degree of your [liability](/liability) for an unauthorized not entirely settled by how expeditiously you tell the bank that unauthorized activity has happened in your account. Informing PayPal rapidly when you have concerns will assist with limiting your liability, and it is suggested that PayPal users check their accounts consistently.

### History of PayPal

The principal form of PayPal as we probably are aware was sent off in the late 1990s by Confinity. The company merged with X.com — an online banking company — and officially took the PayPal name in 2000. It broke into the mainstream when it appeared as a payment facilitator for eBay [auctions](/closeout). The service turned out to be famous to the point that eBay chose to obtain PayPal in 2002, making it the official transfer service for its website. In 2015, PayPal was [spun off](/side project) as an independent company. Company shares trade on the Nasdaq under the ticker symbol PYPL.

Over its time, PayPal has acquired different companies that serve various parts of the financial transaction, digital money transfer, and payments markets. A portion of these acquisitions brought technology improvements and extra elements that were incorporated into the PayPal platform as the companies were absorbed. For instance, PayPal purchased BrainTree in 2013, which claims rival service Venmo.

## Types of PayPal Fees

PayPal doesn't charge a [fee](/fee) for purchases. Different fees shift based on the type of service. For example, personal consumers aren't charged for standard transfers done from a [linked bank account](/linked-transfer-account), yet there is a fee of 1% of the transferred balance or a maximum of $10 for instant transfers.

> PayPal charges users a fee for instant transfers.
>

Money can be transferred for free globally in the event that the two players have PayPal accounts. This makes PayPal a competitive force in the world of bank transfers while sending U.S. currency universally. At the point when foreign currency is accepted into another country, PayPal's fees become more comparable to that of a traditional [bank](/bank).

If a consumer in the U.S. makes a domestic transaction through PayPal, there is no fee. In the event that they utilize a credit card, PayPal charges 3.4% plus a fixed fee of 30 pennies. PayPal's fee structure for merchant transactions is not the same as its personal account transfers. PayPal charges somewhere in the range of 1.9% and 3.49% plus a flat rate of 49 pennies in standard fees for merchants selling goods and services online, including eBay dealers and private businesses that utilization PayPal as a payment option.

## Benefits of PayPal

Purchases made with a chosen handful online retailers might incorporate a discount from PayPal or a [cashback](/cash-back) offer or the like. PayPal additionally offers its own credit card through Synchrony Bank. Approved applicants are supplied with MasterCards branded with the PayPal name.

The company likewise finances large purchases, expands [lines of credit](/lineofcredit), and furnishes customers with debit MasterCards which use PayPal balances to pay for purchases in a brick-and-mortar store or to pull out cash. With PayPal being so widely known, there are stores all through the world that will acknowledge PayPal payments for goods or services — some even acknowledge [contactless](/contactless-payment) PayPal payments.
