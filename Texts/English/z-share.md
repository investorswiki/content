# Z-Share
## What Is a Z-Share?

A Z-share is a class of [mutual fund](/mutualfund) shares that employees of the fund's management company are permitted to claim. Employees might have the option to buy Z-shares. They are likewise utilized in employee benefit plans and offered as a part of compensation or through a reward package.

## How Z-Shares Work

Z-shares are commonly no-load funds which can make them an even more alluring investment for employees. They for the most part have no [front-end](/front-endload) or back-end fees. Generally, they likewise have one of the most reduced [expense ratios](/expenseratio). While Z-share investors will pay a similar management and miscellaneous fund expenses as different investors, their expenses normally do exclude distribution or service fees since they are bought and sold straightforwardly through the management company without the contribution of an intermediary.

Like stock options and stock incentive awards, mutual fund companies offer Z-shares as compensation or through a reward package. At times, employers might match the number of shares purchased as a [bonus](/bonus) for employees. Z-shares are held in employee benefits accounts. All Z-share transactions are managed by the fund company which gives reporting to the employees on the investments.

Different parts of Z-shares are like the other share classes in the fund. Z-share assets are pooled by the fund for management and operational economies of scale. Open-end fund Z-shares must be executed at the forward price, which is its next reported [net asset value](/nav).

Overall, mutual fund companies structure their offerings to incorporate Z-shares as an all inclusive incentive. Z-shares are an important instrument that mutual fund companies can use in a wide range of employee compensation. They are additionally utilized comprehensively in employee benefit plans. Z-shares can be an important offering for employees considering long-term employment possibilities. Companies additionally use Z-shares to support employee spirit, loyalty, and long-term career development.
