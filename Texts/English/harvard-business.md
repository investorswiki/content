# Harvard Business School
## What Is Harvard Business School?

Harvard Business School is an internationally famous business school situated at Harvard University in Boston, Massachusetts. Established in 1908, it admits approximately 930 understudies to its MBA program each year and consistently positions among the top business schools in the world.

Notwithstanding its [Master in Business Administration (MBA)](/mba) program, the school additionally offers different other doctoral and executive education programs, some of which are given in joint effort cooperating institutions, for example, Harvard Law School and the John F. Kennedy School of Government.

## Understanding Harvard Business School

Like all institutions, the specific standing of Harvard Business School on domestic and international rankings will fluctuate from one year to another. By the by, it routinely positions in the main five graduate business programs internationally and is without a doubt one of the most outstanding realized business schools in the world.

One justification behind its substantial [brand recognition](/brand-recognition) is the many books and different distributions put forward by the Harvard Business School Publishing Corporation (HBSPC). As well as distributing the insightful works of Harvard Business School's about 250 full-time employees, the HBSPC is additionally responsible for distributing the popular Harvard Business Review.

As is normal of the alleged Ivy League universities, Harvard Business School is exceptionally particular of who it admits into its program. In 2020, for instance, the school dismissed more than 90% of the north of 9,000 applications received by its MBA program. The doctoral program is regularly even more competitive, with a 96% dismissal rate in 2017.

For those understudies whose applications are accepted, the financial cost of attendance can represent a substantial barrier, with the school itself assessing that the cost of attendance for its 2019-2020 MBA program would probably be more than $110,000 each year. Of this, generally $75,000 is inferable from direct tuition fees while the remainder is due to ancillary costs, for example, [health insurance](/healthinsurance), course and program materials, rental accommodation, and other everyday costs.

## True Example of Harvard Business School

The Harvard Business School's stated mission is to teach leaders who have an effect in the world. A few remarkable graduated class incorporate Salman Khan, organizer behind the self-education website Khan Academy; Meg Whitman, former president and CEO of eBay; and James D. Wolfensohn, former leader of the [World Bank](/worldbank). Graduated class and friends of the school have given a high level of support for the school over its time.

Starting around 2021, Harvard Business School had 85,000 living graduated class altogether addressing 173 countries. Among them, the most noticeable industries in which they worked were [portfolio management](/portfoliomanagement), [manufacturing](/manufacturing), professional services, and management counseling; with "General Management" being by a wide margin the biggest occupational area inside these industries.
