# Short Refinance
## What Is a Short Refinance?

Short refinance is a financial term that alludes to the [refinancing](/refinance) of a mortgage by a lender for a borrower right now in default on their mortgage payments. Lenders short refinance a mortgage to assist a borrower with keeping away from [foreclosure](/foreclosure).

Ordinarily, the new loan amount is not exactly the existing outstanding loan amount, and the lender at times excuses the difference. Albeit the payment on the new loan will be lower, a lender some of the time picks a short refinance since it is more financially savvy than foreclosure procedures.

## How a Short Refinance Works

At the point when a borrower can't pay their mortgage, the lender might be forced to dispossess the home. A mortgage, one of the most common debt instruments, is a loan — got by the [collateral](/collateral) of indicated real estate property — that the borrower is obliged to pay back with a predetermined set of payments. Mortgages are utilized by people and organizations to make large real estate purchases without paying the whole value of the purchase front and center. Over a period of numerous years, the borrower repays the loan, plus interest, until at last, they own the property free as bird.

In the event that a borrower can't make payments on their mortgage, the loan goes into [default](/default2). When that occurs, the bank has a couple of options. Foreclosure is the most widely known (and feared) of the lender's options, as it means the lender assumes command over the property, expels the homeowner, and sells the home. Foreclosure, nonetheless, is a long and costly legal cycle that a lender should keep away from in light of the fact that it may not receive any payments for as long as a year subsequent to beginning the foreclosure interaction and it will likewise miss out on fees associated with the strategy.

A short refinance is a solution a few lenders might offer a borrower who is at risk of foreclosure. A borrower may likewise ask for a short refinance. There are advantages for the borrower: A short refinance permits them to keep the home and decreases the amount owed on the property. Sadly, there is likewise a downside on the grounds that the borrower's [credit score](/credit_score) is probably going to drop since they're not paying the full amount of the original mortgage.

## Short Refinance versus Other Foreclosure Options

A short refinance is just one of several alternatives to foreclosure that may be more savvy for the lender. Another potential solution is to go into a [forbearance agreement](/mortgage_forbearance_agreement), an impermanent delay of mortgage payments. The terms of a forbearance agreement are negotiated between the borrower and the lender.

A lender could likewise opt for a [deed in lieu of foreclosure](/deed_in_lieu_of_foreclosure), which requires the borrower to deed the collateral property back to the lender — fundamentally, surrendering the property — in exchange for release from the obligation of paying the mortgage.

## Illustration of a Short Refinance

Suppose that the market value of your home dropped from $200,000 to $150,000, you actually owe $180,000 on the property. In a short refinance, the lender would permit you to take out another loan for $150,000, and you wouldn't need to pay back the $30,000 difference. Besides the fact that you have would a lower [principal](/principal), yet additionally, no doubt, your regularly scheduled payments would be lower, which could assist you with better managing the cost of them.

## Features
- A short refinance can ding a borrower's credit — however so can late or missed mortgage payments.
- A lender might like to offer a short refinance to a borrower as opposed to going through an extensive, costly foreclosure.
- Lenders might think about a forbearance agreement or a deed in lieu of foreclosure, as both might be more financially savvy.
