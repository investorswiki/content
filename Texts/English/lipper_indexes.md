# Lipper Indexes
## What Are Lipper Indexes?

Lipper Indexes are indexes that track the [financial performance](/financialperformance) of various types of managed fund strategies. Each index depends on the performance of the biggest publicly traded funds in the strategy group.

## Grasping Lipper Indexes

Lipper Indexes are developed and managed by Lipper which is owned by Reuters. Lipper oversees indexes for essentially every type of [mutual fund](/mutualfund) strategy in the investable market. Public disclosure of Lipper Index performance is given by the Wall Street Journal and by Barron's.

To build each index, Lipper midpoints the returns of funds in the investable market that are managed to the Index's strategy. Funds utilized for index return estimations are picked by [assets under management](/aum). The number of funds utilized by Lipper to acquire Lipper Index performance differs extensively. Most Lipper Indexes utilize around 30 to 100 funds to acquire index performance.

Lipper Indexes are many times remembered for mutual fund performance reporting. Investment managers might involve Lipper and Lipper Index data in reporting for their clients. A Lipper Index may likewise be utilized as a mutual fund's primary benchmark.

## Lipper Index Analysis

Lipper Indexes can assist with giving retail investors understanding into the best performing strategies as well as the performance of different strategies all through various market time spans. Through this site page, Lipper furnishes investors with data on the best and most exceedingly awful performing indexes across market categories.

One-year Lipper Index returns through July 24, 2021, show the Lipper Upright Growth Fund as the top-performing strategy in the global equity category. This strategy reports a one-year return of 168.7%. In the mean time, the most obviously terrible performing global equity strategy throughout the last year has been the Lipper SGA International Equity Fund, with a one-year return of - 82.02%. It ought to be noticed that performance data changes consistently, and that new funds might be made or more established funds cease to operate over the long run.

In the [bond](/bond) market, the Lipper UBS FI Enhanced Global High Yield is the top-performing strategy with a one-year return of 24.17%. The most terrible performing strategy in the bond market throughout the last year has been the Lipper Intermediate US Government Index. This strategy has a one-year return of - 0.87%.

In the [money market](/moneymarket) category, Lipper Index returns went from 0.2% to 0.01% over the one-year period through Jan. 5, 2018. The top-performing index was the Lipper Transamerica Government Money Market Fund with a return of 0.2%. The most terrible performing fund in the category was the Lipper Legg Mason Partners Premium Money Market Trust with a return of 0.01%

## Highlights
- Contingent upon the specific strategy, somewhere in the range of 30 to 100 individual funds are utilized to measure the index's performance.
- Lipper indexes are benchmarks for actively-managed mutual fund performance, across a scope of fund strategies and asset classes,
- Lipper indexes have become common as a reference while reporting fund performance to investors and analysts.
