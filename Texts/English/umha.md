# Unified Managed Household Account (UMHA)
## What is a Unified Managed Household Account?

A unified managed household account (UMHA) is a privately managed account that merges various unaffiliated products including mutual funds, ETFs, and individual securities. The account permits immediate family individuals, like parents and children, to access the account. This type of account takes into consideration simplicity of administration for the financial institution and greater transparency for the investing family.

## Understanding Unified Managed Household Accounts (UMHA)

Unified managed household accounts (UMHAs) operate on a single, integrated system, which permits a financial advisor to see different assets and product types to make performance reports. This comprehensive view additionally will empower [financial advisors](/financial-advisor) to tweak investment choices for every client with regards to their household and to improve on operational procedures that are commonly complex and unwieldy. In some UMHA programs, clients can make account bunches for every investment goal. The system upholds groupings with individual performance reports, billing and statements.

Since this account offers complete transparency, family individuals can gain a more profound comprehension of their assets. In the mean time, asset management firms can assign a central relationship manager for large families that have accounts situated in various parts of the business. Unified managed household accounts (UMHAs) can streamline desk work, improve on fees and take into consideration more sophisticated tax management.

## Contemplations for an UMHA

Purchasers ought to know about several factors while combining accounts into an UMHA. Frequently, UMHAs are discretionary programs, meaning the financial advisor has the authority to go with investing choices. Likewise with mutual-fund advisory programs, the investor chooses a model in light of risk tolerance and goals, and the firm deals with [rebalancing](/rebalancing) or even changing asset allocations through and through, contingent upon the degree of tact inherent in a specific program. In this manner, an UMHA's performance will rely largely upon the organization's progress in choosing securities and making changes in accordance with the [allocation](/assetallocation). Investors ought to be particular while picking the firm that will deal with their UMHA.

All things considered, in view of the transparency and simplicity of having all securities under one rooftop, investors have an opportunity to earn better risk-adjusted and tax-managed returns due to far reaching oversight, execution, and reporting. They may likewise save money on fees that are commonly charged for each account. Fee discounts might be accessible in view of your household's assets under management.
