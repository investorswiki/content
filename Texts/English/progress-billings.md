# Progress Billings
## What Are Progress Billings?

Progress billings are [invoices](/invoice) mentioning payment for work completed to date. Progress billings are prepared and submitted for payment at various stages during the time spent a major project.

This type of billing is common in projects that last a long time. It permits the person billing — generally a contractor — to fund the project and themselves as the project proceeds.

The progress billings invoice can incorporate the original contract amount, the amount client has paid to date as well as which percentage of the job has been completed. Nonetheless, progress billings can incorporate different things that owners and contractors ought to comprehend and work out before work starts.

## Figuring out Progress Billings

Progress billings permit contractors to bill their clients gradually as the project is in progress. For progress billings to work, the client and contractor must consent to a payment schedule when invoices will be submitted for payment.

They are valuable for long-term projects that frequently accompanied large budgets. Progress billings prevent the client from being required to fund the project upfront. The contractor likewise benefits by getting compensated at customary spans and can likewise pay for expenses, for example, raw materials during the project by invoicing at different stages.

Payments depend on a checked percentage of project completion. As such, the payments may be split as the project progresses in light of specific milestones set by one or the two players. The last, outstanding balance is generally dispatched to the contractor once the project has been completed, and the client is happy with the work.

## What's Included in Progress Billings

The data remembered for progress billings is unique in relation to the regular invoicing practices of many companies. A portion of the financial subtleties could include:

- The total amount of the contract that is due for the project
- Any approved changes as well as the adjusted amount owed
- The total amount billed up to that point
- The current completion percentage for the project
- The leftover balance owed toward the completion of the project

### Schedule of Values

Progress billings incorporate a technique called the schedule of values, which frames the various costs or values for every one of the project's tasks. A schedule of values is common in the construction industry by which owners and contractors cooperate to determine how much will be spent on each phase of the project. During the progress billings process, a value is assigned to each phase as part of the schedule. Additionally, the completion percentage can be laid out for each phase as progress is made on the overall project.

The schedule of values additionally assists with determining whether there were cost invades or the project went under budget. For instance, the schedule of values would show what was paid for each task as well as the initial estimate. Accordingly, it tends to be determined when in the construction phase did the project surpass the estimated project cost.

Having a schedule of values remembered for the progress billings process assists contractors and owners with fostering a transparent interaction where the financial subtleties are all known upfront. It likewise safeguards construction companies legally and financially by having the estimates recorded as a hard copy with the goal that there are no curve balls toward the completion of the project.

### Percentage Retained

In certain projects, a specific amount or percentage may be withheld by the owner until the completion of the project. The retention amount or retainage can be 5% to 10% of the total project or for each progress value. Basically, the money is held in reserve in the event there are any issues during the project.

A retainage likewise safeguards the owner in case the project isn't completed, the contract is followed appropriately, or on the other hand on the off chance that there are any issues with the contractor and the subcontractors. Be that as it may, the retainage amount can make cash flow issues for the construction company. Subsequently, both the owner and contractor must concoct an agreed-upon retainage amount from the get-go simultaneously.

## Who Uses Progress Billings?

Progress billings are genuinely common in a number of various industries including construction projects. Numerous roofers, handymen, general contractors, painters, electrical experts, and handymen will involve progress billings as part of their organizations. The cost of raw materials, labor, and postpones in construction are a few motivations behind why the industry utilizes progress billings.

They are additionally utilized in aviation and defense since these projects normally have gigantic budgets and can require a very long time to complete. Therefore, progress billing is a natural solution.

## Special Considerations: Factoring in Cost Changes

It is common for a project's cost to change, given the total dollars included and the complexity of the project. The building contract states how clients support cost changes, and regularly, a customer must initial or sign a document that shows the specific changes. Notwithstanding, a few cost invades are undeniable while others are due to a lack of planning. A few common cost invades can include:

- The owner changes the scope of the project task or requests extra work
- Surprising damage to a project or building like termites, shape, or water damage
- Design errors or poor project planning
- Price changes to labor or materials particularly assuming it's an extended project

Numerous contractors factor in a price allowance, for example, a small percentage that gives the ability to increase the price of the project. Owners ought to examine with the contractor the degree of any price allowances.

## Illustration of Progress Billings

When a client picks the contractor, the two will [negotiate](/discussion) the terms of the contract. This interaction incorporates laying out a payment schedule or frequency of payment as indicated by certain milestones agreed upon by the two players. When the work starts, and the milestones are reached, the contractor can then begin submitting invoices to the client.

Expect ABC Construction consents to an arrangement to build an office building for $1.6 million north of a two-year period and that ABC's profit is $600,000.

### Year One
- In year one, 40% of the project is complete
- The company bills the client $640,000 (40% x $1,600,000)
- The company records a profit of $240,000 in year one (40% x $600,000 in total profit).

### Year Two
- In year two, 100% of the project is complete
- The company bills the client the excess 60% owed or $960,000 (60% x $1,600,000)
- The company records a profit of $360,000 in year two (60% x $600,000 in total profit).
