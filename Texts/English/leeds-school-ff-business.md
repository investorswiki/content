# Leeds School of Business
## Leeds School of Business: An Overview

Leeds School of Business is situated at the University of Colorado in Boulder, Colorado. It offers both undergraduate and graduate courses and awards degrees including a Master of Business Administration (MBA) degree program and a Ph.D. program.

Leeds routinely positions among the main 100 b-schools in the U.S. It positions 21st in the **U.S. News and World Report 2021 Best Undergraduate Business Programs**. It positioned 60th on the 2019 **Forbes** rundown of business schools.

## Leeds School of Business In-Depth

Established in 1906 as the University of Colorado's College of Commerce, the college received its current name in October 2001 after a $35 million donation by the family of businessman and giver Michael Leeds.

As part of this donation, the Leeds family ordered that all students at the Leeds School of Business receive significant training in social responsibility, business ethics, diversity and inclusion, and sustainability. An undergraduate student can earn a certificate in socially-dependable enterprise, while executive education students can study corporate social responsibility theory.

The school likewise embraces global commerce, with programs including entry level positions abroad.

Starting around 2021, the school presently has around 3,400 full-time undergraduates and around 400 graduate students enrolled.

> ### Significant
> The Leeds School of Business is accredited by the Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business (AACSB). Of in excess of 16,000 degree-conceding business schools in the world, less than 5% have earned AACSB certification.
>

Today, the Leeds School offers undergraduate degrees in accounting, finance, marketing, and business venture. MBA programs incorporate a full-time option as well as an evening MBA program intended for students currently actively working. Classes on tax planning are led jointly with the university's College of Law.

Master's degree programs are offered in real estate, business analytics, and supply chain management.

### Leeds School of Business by the Numbers

Because of the Leeds School's status as part of a public university system, tuition is rather lower than at numerous private business colleges. For the 2021 scholastic year, MBA graduate program costs were $525,862 for full-time in-state students and $73,259 for out-of-state students.

The median GMT score of students is 610, as per **Forbes**.

The college gauges that 53.9% of its graduates have a job on graduation. The average postgraduate salary starting around 2018 was $100,000.

### Possibilities

By and large, the Leeds School of Business MBA program has been an effective conduit for experts seeking a career in accounting and other financial services disciplines, which in recent years have addressed around 30% of the job situations of MBA graduates. Sales and marketing is another huge industry area, addressing around 25% of job situations.

Graduated class told Forbes they particularly valued the "360-degree enterprising inundation" of its program. The school's closeness to the Colorado tech startup scene was refered to as a bonus, also its stupendous Rocky Mountains location.
