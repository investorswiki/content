# Soci\u00e9t\u00e9 Anonyme (S.A.)
## What Is a Soci\u00e9t\u00e9 Anonyme (S.A.)?

**Soci\u00e9t\u00e9 anonyme** (S.A.) is a French term for a [public limited company](/plc) (PLC) and has numerous equivalents everywhere. A S.A. is the equivalent of a corporation in the United States, a public limited company in the United Kingdom, or a [Aktiengesellschaft](/ag-aktiengesellschaft) (AG) in Germany.

A S.A. is a type of business structure that lays out a company as a legal person that can claim and transfer property, enter contracts, and be held at risk for crimes. A portion of its key benefits are that it limits the owners' personal liability for the company's actions and guarantees the continuity of the moral entity with next to no reliance on the founders, owners ,or shareholders' lives.

## Figuring out the Soci\u00e9t\u00e9 Anonyme

The soci\u00e9t\u00e9 anonyme is a well known business structure with equivalents in numerous different languages and countries. In all cases, a company designated S.A. safeguards its owners' personal assets against claims by creditors, which makes numerous people more ready to begin companies, as it limits their risk.

The S.A. structure likewise makes it more straightforward to meet a developing business' capital-subsidizing needs, as various investors can contribute large or small amounts of money as shareholders in the event that the company settles on public ownership. The S.A. is consequently a key part of a robust capitalist economy.

## History of Soci\u00e9t\u00e9 Anonyme (S.A.)

On Jan. 1, 1808, the French government put into effect the provisions with respect to the formation and structure of a soci\u00e9t\u00e9 anonyme as part of the country's new code directing commerce. One purpose of these new regulations was to forestall the uncontrolled [speculation](/speculation) that had shaken the French markets prior to and during the French Revolution. The code recognized three forms of business organization: the **soci\u00e9t\u00e9 en nom collectif,** the **soci\u00e9t\u00e9 en commandite,** and the soci\u00e9t\u00e9 anonyme.

A soci\u00e9t\u00e9 en nom collectif is a standard partnership where all partners have [unlimited liability](/unlimited-liability), any partner could act for the others, and all partners are actively engaged in the enterprise.

By comparison, the soci\u00e9t\u00e9 en commandite is included limited partners (otherwise called dozing or [silent partners](/silentpartner)) and active partners. The limited partners give assets or capital to the enterprise and have limited liability, while the active partners are responsible for all management activities and have unlimited liability.

## Requirements of a Soci\u00e9t\u00e9 Anonyme (S.A.)

A S.A. is subject to various tax regulations than a [sole proprietorship](/soleproprietorship) or partnership, and, on account of a public S.A., different accounting and auditing requirements. Likewise, to be substantial, a soci\u00e9t\u00e9 anonyme must meet certain requirements. While these requirements differ contingent upon the country, most S.A.s must file [articles of incorporation](/articlesofincorporation), have a board of directors, a managing director or a management board, a supervisory board, a statutory auditor and deputy, a unique name, and some base amount of capital. It is generally comprised for quite some time maximum.

In Luxembourg, for instance, a S.A. must be funded with something like \u20ac30,000, 25% of which must be kept during the incorporation interaction, and have somewhere around two [partners](/partnership). A S.A. in Luxembourg must likewise pay different costs, for example, notary fees, the cost of registration with other regulatory agencies, as well as statutory auditor fees. Notable companies that are laid out as S.A.s incorporate Nestl\u00e9, Anheuser-Busch InBev, and L'Or\u00e9al.
