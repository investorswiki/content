# Polarized Fractal Efficiency (PFE)
## What Is Polarized Fractal Efficiency (PFE)?

The term polarized fractal productivity (PFE) alludes to a [technical indicator](/technicalindicator) that is utilized to determine the [price efficiency](/price-proficiency) for an investment over a client characterized period. It vacillates between - 100 and 100 where the centerline is at zero. Traders can utilize the PFE to see where a security they're keen on is going. The price shows a vertical trend on the off chance that the PFE goes over zero and a descending trend assuming it dips under zero. The indicator was developed by creator, engineer, developer, and trader Hans Hannula.

## How the Polarized Fractal Efficiency (PFE) Works

The polarized fractal productivity indicator was developed by creator, engineer, developer, and trader Hans Hannula. The thought was first referenced in 1994 in the January issue of **Technical Analysis of Stocks and Commodities** magazine. It varies between - 100 and +100, with zero as the centerline. Securities with a PFE greater than zero [trend](/trend) up, while a perusing of under zero shows a descending trend.

The PFE indicator measures the strength of a trend by its position relative to the zero line. When in doubt, the further the PFE value is away from zero, the stronger and more efficient the given trend is. A PFE value that vacillates around the zero line could demonstrate that the [supply and demand](/law-of-supply-demand) for the security are in balance and the price might trade sideways.

Strategies that generally utilize the PFE as a signal consider a buy sign as a reversal toward the indicator and its movement from its base value to zero. A signal to close a position emerges as the value of the indicator arrives at its top over zero. An indicator shift from top to zero presents a sell signal. As a rule of thumb, traders ought to [buy to cover](/buytocover) all [short positions](/short) after the indicator forms another base.

The PFE indicator likewise surveys the strength of a market trend. Accordingly, the higher the indicator value, the stronger the trend. This means 100 shows a strong [uptrend](/uptrend), while a value of - 100 demonstrates a strong downtrend.

> The signature characteristic of the PFE is its utilization of [fractal](/fractal) calculation in determining how efficiently a security's price is moving.
