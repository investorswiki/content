# INSEAD
## What Is INSEAD?

One of the world's top graduate business schools, INSEAD, has campuses in France, Singapore, San Francisco, and the Middle East. Established in 1957, INSEAD is an abbreviation for Institut Europ\u00e9en d'Administration des Affaires.

Today, INSEAD is recognized around the world as one of the top business schools in the world. The school has in excess of 165 employees from 41 countries, with enrollment coming to as high as 1,300 understudies in its different degree programs every year. Furthermore, in excess of 11,000 executives partake in INSEAD's executive education programs every year.

## History of INSEAD

INSEAD was laid out in 1955 when the Paris Chamber of Commerce chose to begin an European business school. After four years, it took in its most memorable MBA understudies. In 1960, 52 understudies from 14 unique countries graduated from the school's MBA program. In 1968, INSEAD started offering its executive program interestingly. The school's Ph.D. program was sent off in 1989.

The school opened its Asian campus in Singapore in 2000 and its Middle East campus in Abu Dhabi in 2010. In 2020, INSEAD announced another hub located in San Francisco, CA to lay out a North American presence.

## INSEAD Campuses

The school's primary campus in France is located in Fontainebleau close to Paris. The subsequent campus, located in Asia, is in Singapore in the Buona Vista district, a short ways from the financial district. The third campus, in Abu Dhabi in the United Arab Emirates, was introduced in 2010.

## Programs Offered at INSEAD

INSEAD offers various graduate and doctoral programs. Notwithstanding the standard educational plans offered below, the school likewise offers redid programs as a team with corporations seeking to grant executive education to their managers.

### MBA

INSEAD offers a 10-month, completely sped up term of study for a [MBA](/mba) with consumption in January and August. Understudies are required to take core subjects including finance, economics, accounting, business ethics, and marketing, as well as electives from a pool of 75 different course offerings. Generally 92% of graduates were employed in their field three months after graduation.

### Global Executive MBA (GEMBA)

This program is offered at the school's three campuses for experienced executives. An intensive 14-to 17-month secluded program, it includes group training, team activities, and 360-degree evaluations. The 2022 class has 190 understudies, each with an average of 13 years of work experience.

### Executive Masters in Change

This program traverses 18 months with eight modules that last three to four days each. Understudies center around human behavior and organizational dynamics. The objectives of the program incorporate learning how to increase mindfulness, turning into an effective agent for organizational change, learning new training systems, and getting a better comprehension of organizational behavior. The class of 2022 and 2023 has 90 participants across the three campuses.

### Executive Masters in Finance

This program is for working experts and lasts 18 months. Admission is on the Asia campus. The program comprises of six modules that last fourteen days each, occurring three to four months separated. The class of 2023 has 30 participants who had an average of around 10 years of work experience.

### Tsinghua-INSEAD Executive MBA (TIEMBA)

This program comes because of a partnership among INSEAD and Tsinghua, a university situated in Beijing. Understudies can seek after this degree at the three INSEAD campuses or at Tsinghua.

### Ph.D. in Management

The most thorough degree offered is a five-year program that prepares understudies who wish to work in academia in business. The initial two years are dedicated to course study, while the leftover three are expected for research and paper work. Consumption occurs in Europe or Asia, with an exchange through the Wharton School in the United States.

The program has a maximum of 18 open places every year. Cooperation is supported between various academic areas to offer more depth for research. Understudies are given lucrative financial support which includes scholarships and living allowances.
