# Construction Loan Note (CLN)
## What Is a Construction Loan Note?

A construction loan note (CLN) is a debt obligation utilized for the funding of construction projects like housing developments. Generally speaking, the note issuers repay the note obligation by giving a more drawn out term bond. The proceeds from the bond pay back the debts on the note.

## Understanding a Construction Loan Note

A [loan note](/loan-note) is a legally binding agreement that can be drawn up by both of the contracted gatherings and is substantial until the loan is paid in full. The note empowers a payee to receive payments over a set period. Payments every now and again incorporate interest and end with the satisfaction of the loan.

A construction loan note is a specific type of loan note, which shows up usually at the municipal level. A large city could utilize a construction loan note to finance a city or housing project. On the off chance that a city encounters a boom in population, for instance, it might have to rapidly build extra housing. The project can start off when the municipality issues a construction loan note to the builders. [Cash flow](/cashflow) from the note takes into account construction to quickly begin. The city will then, at that point, issue a drawn out municipal bond to repay the construction loan note.

> A large city might finance a housing development with a construction loan note, which gives money to builders to kick off the project.
>

A municipal bond is a debt security issued by a state or municipality. Urban areas utilize municipal bonds to finance public projects and capital expenditures. These public projects can go from city housing in the above guide to the construction of expressways, extensions, or schools. Municipal bonds are exempt from federal taxes and most state and nearby taxes.

## Construction Loan Note versus Promissory Note

Stretched out starting with one party then onto the next, the construction loan note is itself a type of promissory note. As a monetary instrument, a [promissory note](/promissorynote) contains every one of the terms of the debt. It has a written commitment by one party, like the note's issuer or maker, to pay another party, like the note's payee, an unequivocal sum of money. Like the promissory, the CLN will list the principal amount, interest rate, maturity date, date and place of issuance, and issuer's signature.

Promissory notes happen most usually with funding sources outside banking institutions, like an individual or company. It falls between a [IOU](/iou) and a loan contract in regards to its unbending nature. A promissory note incorporates a specific guarantee to pay, and the means required to do as such. A loan contract, then again, states the moneylender's right to recourse in the event that the borrower defaults.
