# Know Sure Thing (KST)
## What Is the Know Sure Thing (KST)?

The Know Sure Thing (KST) is a momentum [oscillator](/oscillator) developed by Martin Pring to make rate-of-change readings more straightforward for traders to decipher.

## Ascertaining the Know Sure Thing (KST)

The KST is calculated by taking the [simple moving average](/sma) (SMA) of four unique [rate-of-change](/rateofchange) (ROC) periods, adding them together to think of the KST, and making a signal line by taking the 9-period SMA of the KST.

## Figuring out the Know Sure Thing (KST)

The KST indicator can be utilized in similar way as numerous other momentum oscillators, for example, the notable [relative strength index](/rsi) (RSI). Trading signals are generated when the KST gets over the signal line, yet traders may likewise search for convergence and divergence with the price, overbought or oversold conditions, or [crossovers](/crossover) of the center line.

Numerous traders consolidate the KST indicator with different forms of technical analysis to expand their chances of progress. For instance, traders might take a gander at other non-momentum indicators, chart designs, or [candlestick](/candlestick) examples to help in their decision-production.

> In a 1992 **Stocks and Commodities** article, Pring alluded to the indicator as "Added Rate of Change (KST)," however the KST term stayed with [technical analysts](/technical-expert).
>
