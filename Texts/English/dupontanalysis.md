# DuPont Analysis
## What Is the DuPont Analysis?

The DuPont analysis (otherwise called the [DuPont identity](/dupontidentity) or DuPont model) is a system for breaking down fundamental performance promoted by the DuPont Corporation. DuPont analysis is a valuable technique used to decay the various drivers of [return on equity (ROE)](/returnonequity). The decomposition of ROE allows [investors](/investor) to zero in on the key metrics of financial performance individually to recognize qualities and shortcomings.

## Formula and Calculation of DuPont Analysis

## Everything that DuPont Analysis Says to You

A DuPont analysis is utilized to assess the part parts of a company's return on equity (ROE). This allows an investor to figure out the thing financial activities are contributing the most to the changes in ROE. An investor can utilize analysis like this to compare the [operational efficiency](/operationalefficiency) of two comparative firms. Managers can utilize DuPont analysis to recognize qualities or shortcomings that ought to be tended to.

There are three major financial metrics that drive return on equity (ROE): [operating efficiency](/operationalefficiency), asset use effectiveness, and financial leverage. Operating effectiveness is addressed by [net profit margin](/net_margin) or net income partitioned by total sales or revenue. Asset use productivity is estimated by the [asset turnover ratio](/assetturnover). [Leverage](/leverage) is estimated by the equity multiplier, which is equivalent to average assets partitioned by average equity.

## DuPont Analysis Components

DuPont analysis breaks ROE into its constituent parts to figure out which of these factors are most responsible for changes in ROE.

### Financial Leverage

Financial leverage, or the equity multiplier, is an indirect analysis of a company's utilization of debt to finance its assets. Expect a company has $1,000 of assets and $250 of owner's equity. The [balance sheet](/balancesheet) equation will let you know that the company additionally has $750 in debt (assets - liabilities = equity). ## DuPont Analysis versus ROE

The return on equity (ROE) metric is net income separated by shareholders' equity. The Dupont analysis is as yet the ROE, just an expanded form. The ROE calculation alone uncovers how well a company uses capital from shareholders.

With a Dupont analysis, investors and analysts can dive into what drives changes in ROE, or why a ROE is viewed as high or low. That is, a Dupont analysis can assist with concluding whether its profitability, utilization of assets, or debt that is driving ROE.

## Limitations of Using DuPont Analysis

The greatest drawback of the DuPont analysis is that, while far reaching, it actually depends on accounting equations and data that can be controlled. Plus, even with its breadth, the Dupont analysis needs setting concerning why the individual ratios are high or low, or even whether they ought to be viewed as high or low by any means.
