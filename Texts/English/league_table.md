# League Table
## What Is a League Table?

A league table is a ranking of companies in light of a set of criteria, like revenue, [earnings](/earnings), deals, or some other pertinent [metrics](/metrics). The rankings are organized into records, which can be utilized for investment research purposes or as promotional material for the companies on the rundown.

## Understanding League Tables

League tables are utilized in finance as well as to compare colleges, sports teams, logical data, and different statistics too. For instance, a league table of a college b-ball team could list details from each game, including the name of the rival, points scored, points permitted, the number of team fouls, and whether the outcome was a success or loss.

Probably the most notable league tables in finance are those that track the activities of [investment banks](/investmentbank). The deals directed by investment banks are frequently counted up by value or potentially fees created. There may likewise be a ratio breakdown of the deals, which can incorporate [underwriting](/underwriting) deals, mergers and acquisitions (M&A), and advisory services.

A league table put out by a financial data provider could show all of the [M&A](/mergersandacquisitions) deals that each bank has managed during a yearly period, representing the combined dollar value of the deals as well as the share of the M&A deal market for the period. This data can be important for clients considering investment bank services, as well as to investors quick to dissect investment banks against their friends.

> League tables are created by research organizations for different capabilities and can measure a variety of company metrics across various industries.
>

League tables can likewise be utilized to rank companies by [market share](/marketshare) inside a specific industry. Basically, if there is a statistic someone needs to use to rank companies and they can track down the fundamental data to dissect, a league table of the highest level companies in that category can be made and shared.

## Special Considerations

In practice, league tables can incorporate a large group of data connected with a company, for example, [market capitalization](/marketcapitalization), earnings, and market share. Analysts can then rank or sort the table in view of any of the factors.

While league tables can be valuable in distinguishing a waitlist of companies utilizing a couple of key metrics, it is important to jump into the genuine financials of every one of them before going with an investment choice.
