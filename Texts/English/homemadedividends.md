# Homemade Dividends
## What Are Homemade Dividends?

Homemade dividends are a form of [investment income](/investmentincome) created from the sale of a portion of a singular's investment [portfolio](/portfolio). These assets vary from the traditional [dividends](/dividend) that a company's board of directors conveys to certain classes of shareholders.

## Figuring out Homemade Dividends

The ability of investors to make their own homemade dividends has incited inquiries concerning whether traditional dividends offer real value. Some investment experts contend that since a stock price will diminish by exactly the amount of the dividend on its [ex-dividend date](/ex-dividend), it kills any financial gains.

This thought sits at the core of the [dividend irrelevance theory](/dividendirrelevance), which claims that investors fundamentally don't have to pay regard to a company's [dividend](/dividend) payment policy, since they hold the option of selling off portions of their [equity](/equity) portfolios, would it be a good idea for them they at any point need to produce cash. Doubters of this theory counter-contend that when an investor sells a portion of their portfolio, they end up with less shares, which therefore brings about an exhausted asset base, regardless of any short-term monetary gains they might appreciate.

> Financial specialists [Merton Miller](/merton-miller) and [Franco Modigliani](/franco-modigliani) were among the principal voices to uphold the irrelevance of corporate dividends when they advertised their speculations in the mid 1960s.
>
## Traditional Dividends

As noticed, a company's board of directors is accused of the responsibility of pronouncing dividend payouts to shareholders. Following the [declaration date](/declarationdate), the company lays out a [record date](/recorddate) to determine which shareholders are eligible to receive distributions. The [ex-dividend date](/ex-dividend), which happens unequivocally two business days before the record date, signifies the last day a seller is as yet qualified for collect dividends, even on the off chance that they have proactively sold their shares to a buyer.

Normal dividends commonly happen on a standard month to month or quarterly basis, while extra or [special dividends](/specialdividend) are one-time distributions. Generally talking, a company's board declares special dividends in the wake of seeing exceptionally strong earnings results or when a company looks for either to tangibly change its [financial structure](/financial-structure) or to veer off a [subsidiary](/subsidiary) company.

Companies with sector exposure to essential materials, oil and gas, financials, healthcare, drugs, and utility worries generally produce the highest dividend yields. Besides, companies structured as master limited partnerships (MLPs) or real estate investment trusts (REITs) are likewise top dividend payers, on the grounds that these companies are commonly mature and they exhibit stable cash flows.

Interestingly, new businesses and other high-development companies, for example, numerous technology plays, only occasionally offer high dividends. These companies for the most part really like to reinvest any earnings they make into research and development or to the expansion of operations.
