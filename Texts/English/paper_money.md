# Paper Money
## What Is Paper Money?

Paper money is a country's official, paper currency that is circled for the transactions engaged with gaining goods and services. The printing of paper money is regularly regulated by a country's central bank or treasury to keep the flow of funds in accordance with [monetary policy](/monetarypolicy).

Paper money will in general be refreshed with new adaptations that contain security highlights and endeavor to make it more hard for forgers to make unlawful duplicates.

## Figuring out Paper Money

The main recorded utilization of paper money was suspected to be in the country of China during the seventh century A.D. for the purpose of decreasing the need to carry heavy and unwieldy strings of metallic coins to conduct transactions. Like setting aside an installment at a modern bank, people would transfer their coins to a dependable party and afterward receive a note indicating how much money they had deposited. The note could then be reclaimed for currency sometime in the not too distant future.

## Special Considerations

While paper money is the most accepted medium of exchange, companies frequently issue shares of their own company to purchase different companies and reward their staff. Shares are units of ownership in a company that qualifies the shareholder for an equivalent distribution in any profits. Of all accepted mediums of exchange, shares are nearest to paper money since they can be exchanged on the open market for cash.
