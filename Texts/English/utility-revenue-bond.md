# Utility Revenue Bond
## What is Utility Revenue Bond?

A utility revenue bond, otherwise called an essential service bond, is a type of [municipal bond](/municipalbond) issued to finance a public utility that reimburses [bondholders](/bondholder) straightforwardly from project revenues instead of a general tax fund.

## Understanding Utility Revenue Bond

A utility revenue bond is utilized to fund capital projects in regions considered essential to public services including medical clinics, fire service, water and waste treatment facilities, and improvements to the electrical grid. These services generate revenue through customer fees, which give cash flows that can service the debt.

Revenue bonds accompany either a [gross revenue](/grossrevenuepledge) or a [net revenue](/netrevenuepledge) pledge. A gross revenue pledge gives bondholder payments need over operational or maintenance costs incurred by the project. A net revenue pledge takes into consideration the disbursement of administrative expenses and upkeep costs prior to fulfilling obligations to bondholders.

Utilities convey essential services like water and power. Due to the necessity of these services, it is common for utility revenue bonds to feature a net revenue pledge, as upkeep must be carried out to keep up with them ready to go.

It is likewise common for utilities to be required to keep a specific revenue-to-use ratio. The inclusion of debt repayment is as an expense, and the ratio is in many cases used to support customer rate increments for public utilities.

## Paying Back Utility Revenue Bonds

Municipal bonds reimburse bondholders through either municipal taxation, as in a [general obligation bond](/generalobligationbond), or through [revenue bonds](/revenuebond). Revenue bonds get income from the capital project. The issuer of general obligation bonds guarantees the repayment of debt through any means vital. To collect funds, the issuer can increase government rates, issue one more round of bonds, or even sell physical assets. The issuer isn't compelled to a single revenue stream to fulfill obligations. Investors ought to know about this differentiation and can involve it as they build a diversified, fixed-income portfolio.

Different factors become an integral factor when credit ratings agencies or investors assess utility revenue bonds and the projects they finance. The [coverage ratio](/coverageratio) is utilized to decide expected revenues to principal and interest obligations. Population size and trends can give a thought of future revenue growth or decline for a utility project.

Customer concentration portrays the mix of consumers whose utilization fees support debt repayment. Assuming that a small number of consumers utilize a critical portion of a public utility's service, that might lead to risk for the feasibility of that project's revenues.
