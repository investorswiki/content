# Any-Occupation Policy
## What Is an Any-Occupation Policy?

An any-occupation policy is a type of [disability insurance](/disability-insurance) that classifies the sort of work for coverage purposes. Any-occupation coverage accommodates when the insured is unable to work in a job that is actually suitable for them in light of their education, experience, and age.

An any-occupation policy phrasing, as utilized in disability insurance policies, recognizes the conditions under which a policyholder receives benefits.

The any-occupation terms in the policy determine the type or nature of work that a policyholder can perform. On the off chance that they are capable of as yet working, even assuming it is at a lower-paying job, an any-occupation policy wouldn't pay benefits. Interestingly, the [own-occupation policy](/ownoccupation_policy) is one that would consider the policyholder disabled assuming they are unable to perform similar job as they did before an accident or injury.

For instance, under an any-occupation policy, a specialist who harmed their hands wouldn't receive benefits on the off chance that they might in any case fill in as a doctor in the medical field, yet not as a specialist. Under the own-occupation, they would keep on getting benefits until they could return to rehearsing a medical procedure once more.

Be that as it may, assuming their disability prevents the policyholder from performing any occupation for which they are sensibly qualified, they would fit the bill for benefits. Returning to the case of the specialist, in the event that they couldn't function in a hospital setting without particular equipment or assistance the benefits will proceed.

## Own-Occupation Policy

Own-occupation coverage is for the insured's specific occupation with no different limitations. Assuming that they are unable to perform the material and substantial duties of their pre-injury occupation, the benefit is paid, whether or not they decide to work somewhere else.

For instance, in the event that the pre-disability specialist found another calling as a marketing director for a software company, they would in any case receive the full benefit. If the [insured](/insurance) meets the definition of totally disabled and becomes employed in another occupation, their total disability benefit won't be impacted or decreased by any income from the new calling, no matter what the amount.

Temporary own-occupation coverage is own-occupation with an adjusted benefit. Assuming the insured decides to work in any other occupation, the earnings from the new calling could reduce the benefit amount. A few insurers offer a reward hybrid form of disability insurance. Toward the beginning of getting benefits, the own-occupation definition guides payments. Following a defined period of months or years, the coverage changes to a stricter definition of occupation. For instance, it might possibly keep on paying the benefit if the insured remaining parts unable to work at a calling for which they are qualified and works at no other occupation.
