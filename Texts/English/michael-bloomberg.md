# Michael Bloomberg
## Who Is Michael Bloomberg?

Michael Bloomberg is a billionaire businessman, distributer, donor, and a former three-term chairman of New York City. The founder and owner of Bloomberg LP, he is quite possibly of the wealthiest individual in the world, with an estimated net worth of $82 billion as of June 23, 2022, as per **Forbes**. On Nov. 24, 2019, Bloomberg entered the 2020 race for president of the United States as a Democrat, yet in March 2020, he suspended his campaign and backed President Joseph Biden.

Bloomberg is the founder and owner of [Bloomberg LP](/bloomberg), a financial data and media company. He presently claims 88% of the company.

## Early Years and Education

Bloomberg was brought into the world on Feb. 14, 1942, in Boston, and experienced childhood in adjacent Medford, Massachusetts. He earned an undergraduate degree in electrical engineering from Johns Hopkins University in 1964. He paid his tuition by working as a parking parcel specialist and taking out loans. He earned a MBA from Harvard Business School in 1966.

Bloomberg started his career in financial services in 1966 at the now-defunct Wall Street investment bank [Salomon Brothers](/salomon-brothers), where his most memorable job was including bonds and stock certificates in the bank's vault. He climbed to bond trading, turning into a partner in 1972 and a general partner in 1976.

In 1979, Salomon Brothers moved him from his position of head of equity trading and sales to run Information Systems. This was apparently a demotion, yet it put Bloomberg in charge of the department that executed computer technology. At the point when the company was acquired by the commodity trading firm Phibro in 1981, Bloomberg received a $10 million severance package.

## Bloomberg LP

Bloomberg utilized the windfall to found a company called Innovative Market Solutions that utilized the most recent data systems technology to provide traders with data on U.S. Treasury bond prices. Merrill Lynch turned into a major client and investor in 1982. This company developed into what is today Bloomberg LP, a financial data and media company headquartered in New York City with offices in 100 urban communities around the world.

Bloomberg LP recorded $11 billion in revenue in 2022. The company works data terminals utilized all through the financial services industry. It likewise includes the business news cable channel Bloomberg Television, Bloomberg Radio, and a month to month magazine, **Bloomberg Markets**.

**BusinessWeek** magazine was purchased by Michael Bloomberg's company in 2009 and was renamed **Bloomberg BusinessWeek.**

## Bloomberg's Political Life

Before entering politics, Bloomberg was a Democrat. He changed to the Republican Party to run for chairman of New York City. He won his initial term as city hall leader weeks after the Sept. 11, 2001, psychological oppressor assaults on New York. He won a second term in 2005. After effectively engineering a change in the city's term limits law, he was chosen for a third term, this time as an independent candidate, in 2009.

During his experience as chairman, Bloomberg zeroed in on further developing the city's troubled public school system and renewing its former industrial areas. He was among the main American government officials to force limits on smoking, executing a citywide smoking ban in indoor offices and eateries. He gained nationwide scorn from conservatives for endeavoring to confine the size of sweet soft drinks sold in New York City.

Bloomberg was scrutinized for being withdrawn from his regular constituents and the media reported that the wealth gap in New York City developed during his experience as chairman. The Bloomberg administration was likewise condemned for its "pause and search" policy, which many grumbled unreasonably targeted the Black and Latinx populaces in the city.

## Bloomberg's Post-Mayoral Politics

Bloomberg moved to the independent party while as yet filling in as city hall leader of New York. Politically, he is viewed as financially conservative yet socially liberal, which is a mix is more frequently seen in New York City than somewhere else.

In the 2016 election, Bloomberg supported Democratic Party candidate Hillary Clinton over Republican candidate Donald Trump. He denounced Republicans in Congress as "totally carefree" for neglecting to exercise oversight of President Trump.

In 2018, Bloomberg gave $80 million to Congressional candidates, with everything except a small portion of that total going to Democratic or liberal candidates. His goal in making those donations was to help Democrats gain a majority in the House of Representatives. "I've never believed that the public is very much served when one party is totally out of power, and I think the past eighteen months has been evidence of that," Bloomberg stated.

Bloomberg has been an active defender of firearm control. He was one of 15 American city chairmen who founded an advocacy group, Everytown for Gun Safety, in 2006 to press for reform of firearm laws. He has additionally made substantial donations to environmental organizations and, as city chairman of New York, pressed for clean energy policies.

## Presidential Candidate

On Nov. 24, 2019, Michael Bloomberg entered the 2020 race for the U.S. presidency as a Democrat. He self-funded his campaign and reported to the Federal Election Commission he had spent more than $1 billion.

In his campaign statement, Bloomberg focused on President Trump as his motivation for running. He stated, "I'm running for president to defeat Donald Trump and revamp America. We can't bear the cost of four additional long periods of President Trump's crazy and unethical activities. He addresses an existential threat to our country and our values. Assuming he wins one more term in office, we might in all likelihood never recuperate from the damage."

Bloomberg exited the presidential race on March 4, 2020, after a poor appearance on Super Tuesday.
