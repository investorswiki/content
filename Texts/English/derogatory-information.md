# Derogatory Information
## What is Derogatory Information?

Derogatory information is negative information on an individual's credit report which can be utilized to turn down a loan application. Derogatory information can allude to various things reported to the [credit bureaus](/creditbureau) from credit card companies, lending institutions, and mortgage suppliers.

## Figuring out Derogatory Information

Derogatory information is any reported credit information that can be utilized to deny an individual a loan. When in doubt, derogatory information stays on an individual's credit report for a considerable length of time. Notwithstanding, there are special cases, including insolvencies, after which derogatory information might stay for quite some time.

The most common form of derogatory information is late payments. A creditor can report a payment late at 30 days past due, and afterward increase 30 extra days as the delinquency proceeds.

Collections are one more sort of derogatory information. Following an account spends 120 days overdue, a creditor might sell it to a [collections agency](/collectionagency). This will add extra derogatory information to a credit report on top of the generally reported late payments. [Foreclosures](/foreclosure) are one more form of derogatory information on an individual's credit report. Foreclosure alludes to the legal cycle through which a lender seizes and sells property after an owner defaults. Derogatory information likewise incorporates bankruptcy, tax liens, loan and credit defaults, and civil decisions.

Derogatory information in a credit history can essentially impact your credit score, and make it hard to get new lines of credit, get approved for a loan, or fit the bill to rent a loft. However a few lenders might in any case stretch out a credit extension to an individual with derogatory information on their credit report, it might incorporate higher interest rates or fees.

The main three credit bureaus are Equifax, TransUnion, and Experian.

Derogatory information showing up on a credit report relates to just legal motivations to limit or turn down regarding credit. Financial institutions likewise can't turn down regarding credit in light of age, nor on the grounds that the candidate gets public assistance.
