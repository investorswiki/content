# University of Miami Patti and Allan Herbert Business School
## What Is the University of Miami Patti and Allan Herbert Business School?

The University of Miami Patti and Allan Herbert Business School — frequently alluded to just as the "Miami Herbert Business School" — is a business school situated at the University of Miami in Coral Gables, Florida.

Established in 1929, the school offers a combination of undergraduate and graduate programs, including the [Master of Business Administration (MBA)](/mba). The school received its current name in 2019, in recognition of the various gifts given to the school by its graduated class, Patti and Allan Herbert. Prior to this change, it was known as the University of Miami School of Business Administration.

## Understanding the Miami Herbert Business School

The Miami Herbert Business School offers both undergraduate and graduate degrees. Its undergraduate understudies can pursue 14 unique majors, in subjects, for example, [accounting](/accounting), [economics](/economics), finance, [marketing](/marketing), and [real estate](/realestate). These can likewise be combined with different subjects in an assorted scope of disciplines outside of business, like those in the Arts and Science resources.

The university puts a strong accentuation on international experience among its undergraduate understudies. This is achieved through an assortment of foreign-exchange programs and partnerships with foreign universities. By exploiting these programs, Miami Herbert Business school understudies can complete arranged semesters in areas like Italy, China, South Africa, and, surprisingly, Ecuador's well known Galapagos Islands.

At the graduate level, the Miami Herbert Business School is known for its MBA program, which is routinely positioned among the main 100 MBA programs worldwide by organizations like BusinessWeek, Forbes, and The Financial Times.

## Real World Example of the Miami Herbert Business School

Today, the Miami Herbert Business School is home to around 3,200 understudies, of whom around 900 are graduate understudies. Upon graduation, its understudies' most common employers are in the financial services, [real estate](/realestate), and [health care](/health_care_sector) sectors. With an average starting salary of about $80,000, its full-time MBA graduates are commonly employed in jobs like general management, finance and accounting, and marketing and sales.

The Miami Herbert Business School graduated class community is more than 45,000 strong, with numerous outstanding individuals. These incorporate Raul Alvarez, former President of McDonald's (MCD); Jack Creighton, former CEO of United Airlines (UAL); and Joseph J. Echevarria, Jr., former CEO of Deloitte.
