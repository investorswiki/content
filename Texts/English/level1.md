# Level 1
## What Is Level 1?

Level 1 is a type of trading screen utilized with stock trading that shows the best bid-offer-volume quotes in real-time, or the [national best bid and offer](/nbbo) (NBBO). Level 1 quotes supply fundamental data that, generally, is above and beyond for most investors, however a few very active traders lean toward [order book](/order-book) and market depth data that can be found in higher-level quotes.

The U.S. stock market has three tiers of quotes: Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3. Taking a gander at these quotes permits an investor to perceive how a specific stock is performing after some time as well as where the market action is consolidating.

## Grasping Level 1

Level 1 quotes were generally rare before the approach of the internet and online trading, however are currently widely offered, and investors can access them for free. These quotes can be found on brokers' sites as well as across financial news and media entries, like [Morningstar](/morningstarinc) or Yahoo! Finance. The data is much of the time gave straight by an exchange or through a data broker intermediary.

Reliable Level 1 quotes aid investors in getting better prices for security purchases and sales, particularly in fast-moving markets, where investors might lean toward [limit orders](/limitorder) as opposed to [market orders](/marketorder).

For instance, an investor hoping to purchase $1,000 worth of a stock might check the Level 1 quote to check whether they will actually want to purchase the whole amount at a given price or on the other hand in the event that their order will probably be executed at a higher price.

Level 1 quotes are many times enough for [long-term investors](/longterminvestments) that couldn't care less about the price evolving somewhat. Notwithstanding, [active traders](/active-trading) hoping to capture a lot more modest gains frequently utilize Level 2 quotes so they can accumulate more data.

## Quote Levels
- **Level 1** **quotes** give essential price data to a security including the best [bid and ask](/bid-and-ask) price + [size](/bidsize) on each side.
- **Level 2** **quotes** give more data than Level 1 quotes by adding [market depth](/marketdepth). Level 2 shows market depth ordinarily up to the 5-10 best bid and offer prices.
- **Level 3** **quotes** add greater market depth by giving up to 20 of the best bid and ask prices. Users can likewise enter data straightforwardly. These are principally utilized by brokers and [market makers](/marketmaker).

Every one of the three levels of quotes build on top of one another. Level 1 quotes furnish investors with the highest bid and the most reduced ask prices for an individual stock. This will likewise address the latest data for the particular security in light of the order book in an exchange. These types of quotes are the most common and are what individual investors see when they request data from their financial services company. Level 1 quotes give the best real-time bid/ask for a given security.

[Level 2](/level2) quotes go a step further by offering market depth to real-time quotes for every symbol. The additional granularity assists active traders with determining the size of buy and sell orders at various prices (depth) and shows where most orders are concentrated among market producers (order book). This permits investors to distinguish the most impenetrable, least bid/ask spread, which is important for bigger investors who conduct high volume and [high-frequency trades](/high-frequency-trading) (HFT).

For instance, assume that an active trader sees that Acme Co. has a Level 1 quote showing a $5.00 price with a $5.10x100 ask and a $4.90x500 bid. The trader might accept there is strong [support](/support) at $4.90 with an order for 500 shares and somewhat weak [resistance](/resistance) at $5.10 with just 100 shares on the market. Be that as it may, Level 2 quotes might show an order for 1,000 shares at $5.11 and no orders below $4.90 until $4.85, which makes the stock look significantly weaker than the Level 1 quotes would suggest.

There are additionally [Level 3](/level3) quotes, which give all the data and services of Level 1 and Level 2 quotes as well as conceding an investor the ability to enter or change quotes, execute orders, and convey confirmations of trades. These types of quotes are held for registered brokers and financial institutions. Market producers, for instance, participate in Level 3 quotes, which permits them to execute customer orders.
