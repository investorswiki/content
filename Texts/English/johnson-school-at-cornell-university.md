# The Samuel Curtis Johnson Graduate School of Management
## What Is The Samuel Curtis Johnson Graduate School of Management?

The Samuel Curtis Johnson Graduate School of Management — frequently alluded to just as "Cornell Johnson" — is a graduate business school based at Cornell University in Ithaca, New York. Initially established in 1946, it received its current name following a $20 million gift by the family of businessman and [philanthropist](/charity), Samuel Curtis Johnson.

The school is home to approximately 100 employees and offers different very much regarded programs, including a two-year [Master of Business Administration (MBA)](/mba) program, an accelerated 1-year MBA, and a dedicated [Executive MBA](/executive-mba) program.

## How Cornell Johnson Works

The Cornell Johnson MBA program is known for its accentuation on giving an exceptionally vivid experience. This is accomplished through temporary positions, case studies, and visits to important companies. Also, understudies are required to choose an area of concentrate generally right off the bat in the MBA educational program. Instances of these areas of specialization incorporate [investment banking](/investment-banking), strategic operations, [capital markets](/capitalmarkets) and asset management, and digital technology. When picked, understudies benefit from a carefully tailored educational program of scholastic and involved experiences zeroed in on the current [best practices](/best_practices) and emerging difficulties of that sector.

For understudies unable to focus on a two-year MBA, an Accelerated MBA is likewise available. This one-year program is situated toward understudies who as of now have advanced degrees in a logical or technical discipline, like a PhD, JD, or the Chartered Financial Analyst (CFA) assignment. This program permits understudies to take classes at Cornell University's technology-arranged grounds, Cornell Tech, notwithstanding their core MBA courses.

Overall, Cornell Johnson's MBA program has generally performed very well on international [B-School](/b-school) rankings. In 2019, for instance, Forbes rated it as the ninth best MBA program in the world, though U.S. News rated it at #15 worldwide in 2021.

## Real World Example of the Cornell Johnson

As is average of Ivy League schools, the cost of going to Cornell Johnson is among the highest of any business school, with annual tuition of almost $144,000 each year. Simultaneously, Cornell Johnson's graduating class of 2018 received an average starting salary generally equivalent to the annual tuition cost, alongside an average signing bonus of just more than $30,000.

By and large, more than 94% of Cornell Johnson graduates have received job offers in something like 3 months of graduation.

Upon graduation, Cornell Johnson understudies join a graduated class network north of 15,000 strong. Among them are numerous notable members, including Ken Durr, former CEO of Chevron (CVX); Daniel Hesse, former CEO of Sprint Corporation (S); and Anne Chow, CEO of AT&T Business.
