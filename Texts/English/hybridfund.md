# Hybrid Fund
## What Is a Hybrid Fund?

A hybrid fund is an investment fund that is described by diversification among at least two asset classes. These funds regularly invest in a mix of [stocks](/stock) and [bonds](/bond). They may likewise be known as asset allocation funds.

## Figuring out Hybrid Funds

Hybrid funds offer investors a diversified portfolio. The term hybrid demonstrates that the fund strategy remembers investment for numerous asset classes. As a rule, it can likewise mean that the fund utilizes an alternative mixed management approach.

Hybrid funds are commonly known as asset allocation funds. In the investment market, asset allocation funds can be utilized for some reasons. These funds offer investors an option for investing in different asset classes through a single fund.

Hybrid funds developed from the implementation of modern portfolio theory in fund management. These funds can offer fluctuating levels of risk tolerance going from conservative to direct and aggressive.

- **Balanced funds** are likewise a type of hybrid fund. Balanced funds frequently follow a standard [asset allocation](/assetallocation) proportion, like 60/40.
- **Target date funds** or lifecycle funds additionally fit into the hybrid category. These funds invest in various asset classes for diversification. Target date funds fluctuate from standard hybrid funds in that their portfolio portions start with a more aggressive allocation and continuously rebalance to a more conservative allocation for use by a predefined utilization date.
- A [blend fund](/blendfund) (or blended fund) is a type of value mutual fund that incorporates a mix of both [value](/valuestock) and [growth stocks](/growthstock). These funds offer investors diversification among these well known investment styles in a single portfolio.

In all cases, hybrid funds will incorporate some mix of at least two asset classes. In risk-targeted and balanced funds, allocations will normally stay at a fixed proportion. In funds targeting a predetermined utilization date, the proportion of asset mix will change after some time. In the entirety of the funds, the investment manager may actively deal with the individual holdings inside every asset category to answer changing market conditions and potential capital appreciation amazing open doors

## Instances of Hybrid Funds

Investment managers offer a great many options for hybrid funds. Below are two models.

### Vanguard Balanced Index Fund (VBIAX)

This fund has a 60/40 balance among stocks and bonds. The stock portion of the portfolio looks to recreate the CRSP U.S. Total Market Index. The bond portion of the portfolio tries to imitate the Bloomberg U.S. Aggregate Float Adjusted Index. The fund has an expense ratio of 0.06% as of Q2 2021.

### T. Rowe Price Retirement 2060 Fund (TRRLX)

The T. Rowe Price Retirement 2060 Fund is a hybrid target-date fund. As of May 2021, it had over 90% of the portfolio in stocks and roughly 8% in bonds and other fixed-income securities. The fund utilizes a fund of funds approach with 19% of the portfolio in a growth stock fund. The fund has an expense ratio of 0.71% as of Q2 2021.

## Features
- Balanced funds, which hold normally 60% stocks and 40% bonds are a common illustration of a hybrid fund.
- A hybrid fund is a classification of a mutual fund or ETF that invests in various types of assets or asset classes to create a diversified portfolio.
- Blended funds, which mix growth and value stocks, are another hybrid fund model.
