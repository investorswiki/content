# National Futures Association (NFA)
## What Is the National Futures Association (NFA)?

The National Futures Association (NFA) is an independent self-regulatory organization for the U.S. [futures](/futures) and [derivatives](/subordinate) markets. Designated by the Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC) as a registered futures association, the NFA's command is to defend the integrity of the derivatives markets, safeguard investors, and guarantee that members satisfy their regulatory obligations.

## Grasping the National Futures Association (NFA)

The NFA operates at no cost to the taxpayer and is basically financed by membership contribution, fees, and [assessments](/evaluation) paid by members and different users of the derivatives markets.

NFA membership gives assurance to the investing public that all firms, delegates, and associates who conduct business with them on the U.S. futures exchanges must stick to similar high standards of professional conduct. The firms working in the industry must pay membership duty to the NFA, which is the way the NFA gets its money.

The NFA started operating in 1982, subsequent to the foundation of the [Commodity Futures Trading Commission](/cftc) (CFTC) in 1974; this legislation likewise authorized the creation of registered futures exchanges, consequently facilitating the creation of a national self-regulatory organization.

Notwithstanding regulation of the U.S. futures market, the NFA's duties and capabilities incorporate registration, compliance, and [arbitration](/arbitration). It battles [fraud](/fraud) and abuse in the futures markets through a combination of registration requirements, compliance rules, strong enforcement authority, and real-time market surveillance.

## Responsibilities of the National Futures Association

The following are the primary responsibilities of the NFA:

**Registration and Membership:** Firms that conduct derivatives business need to register with the CFTC and most likewise need to register with the NFA. The CFTC has given the NFA registration responsibilities.

**Rulemaking:** This includes concluding what are the industry's best practices and afterward commanding those practices for the whole industry.

**Enforcement and Registration Actions:** When rules are not complied, the NFA makes a disciplinary move against members.

**Member Education and Resources:** The NFA gives educational resources to its members all together that they can comprehend the rules and regulations and how to keep them.

**Arbitration:** Any futures or forex-related debates can be settled through the NFA's arbitration program.

**Financial backer Protection:** The NFA gives investors resources before going with any investment choices.

**Outreach Programs:** The NFA offers an assortment of training programs to elements on request.

**Market Regulation:** Designated contract creators (DCMs) and swap execution facilities (SEFs) can acquire regulatory services from the NFA.

## Member Firms

All futures professionals required to register must go through a foundation investigation before they might register.

As of Jan. 31, 2022, the NFA has 3,117 members. Member categories are as follows:

- **Commodity Pool Operators (CPO):** People or organizations that operate and request funds for a commodity pool.
- **Commodity Trading Advisors (CTA):** People or organizations that exhort clients on derivatives trades.
- **Futures Commission Merchants (FCM):** An entity that acknowledges or solicits trades.
- **Introducing Brokers (IB):** People or organizations that interface clients with a broker.
- **Retail Foreign Exchange Dealer (RFED):** A counterparty to non-U.S. currency transactions.
- **Swap Dealers:** Those who make a market for, and execute in, swaps as their business.
- **Exchanges:** A marketplace where financial instruments are bought and sold.
- **Associates:** Any individual that "solicits orders, customers or customer funds (or who regulates people so connected with) for the benefit of a futures commission merchant (FCM), retail foreign exchange dealer (RFED), introducing broker (IB), commodity trading advisor (CTA) or commodity pool operator (CPO)."
- **Significant Swap Participants:** An individual or entity whose outstanding swaps make "significant counterparty exposure that could affect the financial strength of the United States banking system or financial markets."

The NFA, as a self-regulatory organization, has the power to look for and execute what it accepts are best practices for the industry. The NFA makes rules that its members must follow and has the power to impose fines or disavow the membership (which could close down a business) of its members. It offers an arbitration interaction to assist customers and businesses with resolving questions or come to a resolution on claims of wrong-doing.

## Membership Dues

The NFA has numerous levy and fees and they differ contingent upon the type of member. For instance, the membership levy for a Tier 1 swap dealer are $1.3 million though for a Tier 2 swap dealer the duty are $325,000. For an introducing broker, the contribution are $750 and for an introducing broker swaps firm, the duty are $2,500. These are annual contribution.

> The financial requirement to register with the NFA is an adjusted net capital of $1 million and for a swap dealer it is $20 million.
>

On the off chance that payment is late, there is a fee of $25. In the event that levy are not paid in the span of 30 days of the date they are payable, then, at that point, membership is removed.

## Real-World Example

In 2019, in light of grievances recorded in 2018, East West Global LLC and two individuals at the firm were fined, and one of the individuals had their NFA membership revoked for quite some time.

The firm and the two individuals were charged for utilizing insufficient promotional material, inadequate sales practices, and neglecting to fulfill the high guidelines of commercial honor, among several different charges.

One of the individuals was ordered [jointly, alongside the firm, and severally](/jointlyandseverally) to pay a $75,000 fine. The firm was likewise ordered to fix the issues tended to in the protest.

The other individual, following a settlement offer, agreed not to apply for NFA membership (or associate membership) for quite a long time. Following five years, in the event that he reapplies for membership, he should promptly pay a $90,000 fine.
