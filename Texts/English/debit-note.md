# Debit Note
## What Is a Debit Note?

A debit note is a document utilized by a vendor to educate the buyer regarding current debt [obligations](/commitment), or a document made by a buyer while returning goods received on [credit](/credit). The debit note can give data in regards to an impending [invoice](/invoice) or act as a wake up call for funds currently due. For returned things, the note will incorporate the total anticipated credit, an inventory of the returned things, and the justification for their return.

## How a Debit Note Works

A debit note, otherwise called a [debit memo](/debit-reminder), is generally utilized in business-to-business transactions. Such transactions frequently include an extension of credit, meaning a vendor sends a shipment of goods to a company before the buyer's cost is paid. The note lets the buyer know that the seller has debited their account. Albeit real goods are evolving hands, real money isn't moved until a genuine invoice is issued. Debits and credits are rather signed in an accounting system to follow sent [inventories](/inventory) and payments owed.

Debit notes are separate from invoices since they are generally designed as letters, and they may not need immediate payment. This is true when the debit note is utilized to advise the buyer regarding impending debt obligations in light of amounts that presently can't seem to be officially invoiced.

### Alternate Forms of Debit Notes

A few companies use debit notes to bill for things that are not their primary business. For instance, on the off chance that a company rents a portion of its warehouse space, it could issue a debit note for the rent. Debit notes could likewise be utilized to address botches in invoices. Assuming a client is underbilled on an invoice, for instance, a debit note may be issued for the missing amount that ought to have been billed.

Notwithstanding the letter design, debit notes may likewise be given as delivery receipts received goods. While the amount due might be noted, payment isn't expected until an official invoice is shipped off the buyer. This can permit a buyer the opportunity to return goods, if essential, without first giving payment.

Some debit notes might be sent as instructive postcards that main act as a wake up call of the debt the buyer has accrued. This can be useful in cases where the seller isn't certain on the off chance that an original invoice was received or surveyed. The postcard can likewise contain data on how the debt can be settled, like important contact data.

### Debit Notes as Optional Documents

Not all companies decide to send debit notes to buyers with outstanding or pending debt obligations. Generally, a seller either thinks of it as a standard business practice and uses it as per internal procedures or doesn't utilize it by any means. At times, a buyer can request a document with the data contained in a debit note to meet internal recordkeeping requirements.
