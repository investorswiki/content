# The MSCI All Country World Index (ACWI)
## What Is the MSCI All Country World Index?

The MSCI All Country World Index (ACWI) is a stock index intended to follow broad global equity-market performance. Kept up with by [Morgan Stanley Capital International ](/msci)(MSCI), the index includes the stocks of almost 3,000 companies from 23 developed countries and 25 [emerging markets](/emergingmarketeconomy).

Fund managers utilize the MSCI ACWI as an aide for [asset allocation](/assetallocation) and a benchmark for the performance of global equity funds. The index is additionally utilized as the basis for making investment products, for example, [exchange-traded funds](/etf) (ETFs).

## Grasping the MSCI All Country World Index

Investors can expand their portfolio holdings in a number of ways. A portfolio manager or investor can purchase a scope of individual stocks that have a low or negative [correlation](/correlation) with one another.

Then again, a not so much costly but rather more efficient method for investing may be to claim ETFs that duplicate the performance of an index, for example, the [Dow Jones Industrial Average](/djia) (DJIA) or the [S&P 500 index](/sp500). For investors that likewise need to have exposure to global stocks, the MSCI ACWI index can be a decent option.

[Institutional investors](/institutionalinvestor), including the people who oversee mutual funds and pension funds, can involve the MSCI ACWI as a benchmark to measure the performance of their portfolios and a manual for geographical diversification. Individual investors can likewise involve the ACWI as a benchmark to compare which funds have the best [risk-adjusted returns](/riskadjustedreturn).

## Alternatives to the MSCI ACWI for Global Equities

An investor seeking to [diversify](/diversification) their portfolio geographically could invest a portion of their funds in an ETF that tracks the MSCI ACWI All Cap Index, which has in excess of 15,000 equity holdings from 23 developed markets and 25 emerging markets. This would give exposure to a large number of global stocks yet incorporate a lot more small and mid-cap stocks.

It's important to note that investors ought to know that an index containing foreign equities could likewise contain U.S. stocks. Thus, investors ought to monitor whether they'd be overexposed to U.S. equities on the off chance that they have previously allocated a critical portion of their portfolio to U.S. stocks. If it's not too much trouble, counsel a qualified [financial advisor](/financial-advisor) to determine the best asset allocation strategy for your investment needs and long-term financial objectives.

## Features
- The MSCI ACWI index is utilized as a benchmark for global equity funds and as a manual for asset allocation.
- The MSCI ACWI is a stock index that tracks almost 3,000 stocks in 48 developed and emerging market countries.
- The parts of the MSCI ACWI index have a combined market capitalization of more than $70 trillion.
- Individual investors can gain access to the index through ETFs that reproduce its performance.
