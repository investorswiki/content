# FHA Streamline Refinance
## What Is a FHA Streamline Refinance?

A FHA streamline refinance is an option for homeowners that is administered by the [Federal Housing Administration](/fhaloan) (FHA). It is planned to give consumers a simpler method for getting mortgage refinancing from a participating lender. To qualify, a homeowner must currently have a FHA-protected mortgage and can't be delinquent on the payments.

## How a FHA Streamline Refinance Works

The FHA has been permitting streamlined refinancing of mortgages it guarantees since the 1980s. The two lenders and consumers benefit from a reduced amount of desk work and less required steps to take. The objective, as in any refinance, is to reduce the homeowner's month to month interest and principal payment. Be that as it may, something like $500 in cash can be taken out on the refinanced mortgage.

The FHA doesn't need a [appraisal](/appraisal) on a streamline refinance. It utilizes the homeowner's initial purchase price all things being equal. It likewise may not need a credit report, albeit the bank that is really lending the money may.

FHA-guaranteed mortgage loans are a famous decision for first-time homebuyers since they frequently require more modest down payments and lower credit scores than other conventional mortgages. Nonetheless, these types of mortgages are just offered by banks that are FHA-endorsed.

On signing the mortgage, the borrower consents to pay 1.75% of the loan amount as an upfront fee, otherwise called [up-front mortgage insurance](/up-front-mortgage-insurance-ufmi) (UFMI), and annual fees for mortgage insurance premium (MIP) totaling 0.45% to 1.05%.

> Streamline refinancing cuts out a portion of the desk work and costs for the two lenders and consumers.

## Requirements for a FHA Streamline Refinance

The greatest requirement for a FHA streamline refinance is showing a net unmistakable benefit. In effect, this means that the homeowner must show the FHA that refinancing will be to their quantifiable financial benefit. This net substantial benefit could be accomplished by decreasing the mortgage term, the interest rate, or both.

In this way, for instance, a homeowner wouldn't meet the requirements for a FHA streamline refinance in the event that the new mortgage brings down the regularly scheduled payment exclusively by broadening the total number of payments due. The homeowner will pay a similar amount over the long haul and has not accomplished a net financial benefit.

## Features
- The borrower can't be delinquent on their current mortgage and something like $500 can be removed from the refinance mortgage.
- To meet all requirements for a FHA streamline refinance the borrower must show there will be a net substantial benefit to doing as such.
- The program offers a lower-bother method for refinancing the mortgage, including not needing an appraisal.
- A FHA streamline refinance is accessible to homeowners who as of now have FHA-guaranteed mortgages.
