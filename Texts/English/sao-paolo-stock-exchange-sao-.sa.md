# S\u00e3o Paulo Stock Exchange
## What Is the S\u00e3o Paulo Stock Exchange?

The S\u00e3o Paulo Stock Exchange is a stock and over-the-counter market based in Sao Paulo, Brazil.

Founded in 1890 as Bolsa de Valores de S\u00e3o Paulo (BOVESPA), it is currently part of B3 SA or Brasil, Bolsa, Balc\u00e3o. B3 was formed following the 2008 merger of the exchange with the Brazilian Mercantile and Futures Exchange or Bolsa de Mercadorias e Futuros.

The exchange is among the largest in the Americas with a [market capitalization](/marketcapitalization) of $978 billion and 351 listed companies as of February 2021.

## Understanding the S\u00e3o Paulo Stock Exchange

The S\u00e3o Paulo Exchange was founded in 1890 in S\u00e3o Paulo, Brazil, one of the country's largest and most cosmopolitan cities. It was usually referred to as BOVESPA, an abbreviation for its Brazilian name, Bolsa de Valores de S\u00e3o Paulo.

It is one of five exchanges in the country and among the largest in the Americas. It is one of Brazil's largest companies in market value, holding a prominent global position in the stock market industry.

The S\u00e3o Paulo Exchange merged with the Brazil Mercantile and Futures Exchange in 2008 to create the BM&F Bovespa Exchange. It is presently known as B3 or Brasil, Bolsa, Balc\u00e3o, and trades under ticker symbol B3SA3 on the Novo Mercado premium listing segment. The company has a strategic partnership with the [CME Group](/cme), which permits the cross-listing of different financial products.

A total of 351 companies are listed on the exchange, just four of which are foreign names. The value of share trading for equities on the exchange was $129 billion as of February 2021.

Pre-market trading takes place between 9:45 a.m. also, 10:00 a.m. nearby time, Standard trading takes place between 10:00 a.m. also, ends at 5:00 p.m. or on the other hand 5:15 p.m., depending on the security.

## Requirements for the S\u00e3o Paulo Stock Exchange

The S\u00e3o Paulo exchange lists commodities and futures as well as stocks. Over-the-counter transactions additionally are permitted. Companies are required to follow the corporate governance rules of the segment in which they trade.

Stocks listed on the exchange are represented by [ticker symbols](/stocksymbol) with five characters. The first four characters are capital letters that denote the name of the issuer. The last character indicates the class of the stock:

- 3 represents an ordinary stock
- 4 is a [preferred stock](/preferredstock)
- 5 indicates preferred class A shares
- 6 indicates preferred class B shares
- 7 indicates preferred class C stock
- 8 indicates preferred class D stock

Stock prices are quoted in [Brazilian reals](/brl-brazilian-real) (BRL) to two decimal places and settle in T+2 days.

Foreign companies might list their shares on the exchange utilizing sponsored Brazilian Depositary Receipts (BDRs).

## Special Considerations
### BOVESPA Index

The BOVESPA Index, usually known as IBOVESPA, is the primary performance indicator of stocks traded on the exchange. Since its creation in 1968, the index has been a benchmark for investors around the world who are interested in Brazilian equities.

A five-year charge of the IBOVESPA shows steady improvement in its performance, from 65,403 BRL on April 1, 2017, to 121,570 BRL on April 1, 2022.

A total of 82 companies were included in the index as of April 19, 2021. The IBOVESPA is weighted on a market value of [free float](/freefloatmethodology) and is reconstituted every quarter. It accounts for about 80% of the trades and the financial volume of the [capital markets](/capitalmarkets).

### Commitment to Sustainability

B3 is committed to [sustainability](/sustainability). It was the world's first stock exchange to adhere to the Global Compact in 2004.

The company is likewise a member of the U.N. Global Compact Brazilian Committee. Since 2017, an exchange representative has acted as the vice president of the Global Compact's Brazilian Committee.

Global Compact is a United Nations initiative that plans to mobilize the international business community to adopt business practices that reflect internationally accepted values in regards to human rights, labor relations, the environment, and corruption prevention.

The Global Compact stipulates that companies report their progress yearly, as regards the Ten Principles of the Global Compact, through a Communication on Progress (CoP) on the Global Compact's international website.

## The Bottom Line

Investors interested in any emerging market would do well to closely follow the nation's social and economic progress. Brazil suffered a recession in 2020. Its economy contracted by more than 4%, due mostly to the effects of the COVID-19 pandemic. This was followed by a recovery in 2021. Nevertheless, the nation's fiscal health is considered poor.
