# Deed of Surrender
## What Is a Deed of Surrender?

A deed of surrender is a legal document that transfers property ownership for a given time frame period gave certain conditions are met. A deed of surrender lets one party, like a renter, give up their claims on a specific piece of property to a[ landlord](/landlord) or other party that holds the underlying title. When the deed of surrender has been marked, any remaining claims on the property can be settled.

> A deed of surrender transfers ownership back to the title owner, regularly after fiduciary duties and obligations by the two players have been satisfied.
>
## How a Deed of Surrender Works

A deed of surrender can be utilized to end any commercial property lease or potentially free tenants from their lease obligations. The document might be utilized in situations where the tenant rebuilds their business and needs to go into a lease under the name of the new business entity. In exchange for surrendering their rights to a property, the tenant is released from additional claims and requests by the landlord. The landlord is in like manner released from additional claims and requests by the tenant. The deed of surrender frames each party's rights.

## Requirements for a Deed of Surrender

Deeds of surrender are utilized in circumstances where landlords and tenants have mutually satisfied their [fiduciary](/fiduciary) duties and obligations. In the event that either party penetrated the lease contract, ending the legal relationship turns out to be more convoluted. For instance, on the off chance that a tenant owes several months' back rent, the landlord probably won't execute a deed of surrender. Doing so would surrender their rights to collect back rent.

A deed of surrender frequently states the condition that the tenant must leave the property in. This can incorporate indicating the cleanliness of the property and whether machines ought to be eliminated by the tenant. The last date of occupancy and cutoff time to clear out may likewise be listed. For instance, when a retailer moves out of leased space, the deed of surrender might state that all signage, racks and other equipment the retailer claims must be eliminated from the property.

The deed could specify that infrastructure and conveniences owned by the landlord, for example, light fixtures or an air conditioning system, must stay in place. The deed of surrender would likewise declare when the tenant must have completed any clean up required after the removal of their effects.

The document likewise confirms the tenant satisfied any financial obligations to the landlord, states the landlord returned the tenant's deposit or a portion thereof, or that the tenant isn't due a refund of deposit by any means. The deed of surrender is endorsed by the landlord, the tenant and a witness like a notary public.

## Features
- Deeds of surrender for commercial property determine conditions that the space must be left in while emptying. Conditions with respect to deposits (and refunds) are determined in the deeds of surrender.
- Deeds of surrender — used to transfer ownership of a property for a set period — can be utilized to release retail tenants from their lease obligations, while likewise illustrating extra rights going ahead.
- When the deed of surrender has been marked, outstanding claims on the property can be settled.
