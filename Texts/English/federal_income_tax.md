# Federal Income Tax
## What Is Federal Income Tax?

The U.S. federal income tax is a tax exacted by the [Internal Revenue Service (IRS)](/irs) on the annual earnings of individuals, corporations, trusts, and other legal elements. Federal income taxes apply to all forms of earnings that make up a taxpayer's [taxable income](/taxableincome), including wages, salaries, commissions, bonuses, tips, investment income, and certain types of unearned income.

In the U.S., federal income tax rates for individuals are progressive, meaning that as taxable income increases, so does the tax rate. Federal income tax rates range from 10% to 37% and kick in at specific income limits. The income goes the rates apply to are called [tax brackets](/taxbracket). Income that falls within each bracket is taxed at the relating rate.

The federal corporate tax rate is a flat 21% (decreased from 35% by the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act, passed into law toward the finish of 2017).

## How Federal Income Tax Works

Tax is collected from individuals and [corporations](/corporatetax) by the city, state, or country where they dwell or operate. When the tax collected is credited to the country's government, it is alluded to as a federal tax.

Governments utilize the money collected through federal taxes to pay for the growth and upkeep of the country. Some gander at federal tax as "lease" charged to live in a country or the fee to utilize the resources given by a country. When you pay tax to the U.S. government, you're active putting resources into your economy, as the government utilizes the funds to do the following:

- Build, repair, and keep up with infrastructure
- Fund the pensions and benefits of government workers
- Fund Social Security programs
- Fund major wellbeing programs, including Medicare, Medicaid, CHIP, and marketplace appropriations
- Fund "wellbeing net" programs to help lower-income families
- Fund defense and international security programs
- Further develop sectors like education, wellbeing, agriculture, utilities, and public transportation
- Leave on new accomplishments like space investigation
- Give emergency disaster relief

> In 2021, the U.S. government collected $4.05 trillion in revenue and burned through $6.82 trillion, coming about in a $2.77 trillion deficit.
>

The biggest source of revenue for the federal government comes from the [income](/incometax) of its inhabitants. In 2020, the IRS collected almost $3.5 trillion in receipts, of which individuals, estates, and trusts contributed $1.87 trillion. Meanwhile, business income taxes accounted for about $264 billion.

## Special Considerations

Workers receive their earnings either as [net income (NI),](/netincome) otherwise called [take-home pay](/take-home-pay), or [gross income](/grossincome) (or gross pay). Net income is the total amount received after taxes, benefits, and voluntary contributions are deducted from the paycheck. When taxes are withheld, it means the company or payer has paid the tax to the government on the worker's behalf. Gross income incorporates the total amount of income, and the worker must pay the government what is owed.

> While canceled obligations are typically taxable, Paycheck Protection Program (PPP) loans gave during the COVID-19 pandemic are exempt from federal taxation. In any case, a few states treat the excused amount as taxable income or deny deductions for expenses paid for by the credit — or both.
>

The amount your employer withholds for taxes relies heavily on how much you earn and the information you gave your employer on Form W-4. All money earned, whether as a wage, salary, cash gift from an employer, business income, tips, gambling income, bonuses, or unemployment compensation, comprises income for federal tax purposes.

## State Income Tax versus Federal Income Tax

It is important to recognize the overall thought of income tax and federal income tax. In the U.S., governments at the state level may likewise levy income taxes notwithstanding federal income taxes.

Not all states have [state-level income taxes](/state-income-tax). At present, Alaska, Florida, Nevada, South Dakota, Tennessee, Texas, Washington, and Wyoming don't have an income tax. New Hampshire taxes dividends and interest income just, however the state will totally phase out these taxes by 2027.

## Features
- Federal income tax is utilized for different expenses going from building and repairing the country's infrastructure to further developing education and public transportation and giving disaster relief.
- Federal income taxes are collected by the federal government, while state income taxes are collected by the individual state(s) in which a taxpayer lives and earns income.
- Alaska, Florida, Nevada, South Dakota, Tennessee, Texas, Washington, and Wyoming have no state income taxes.
- The federal income tax is the biggest source of revenue for the U.S. government.
- Federal income taxes depend on your income and filing status; a similar federal tax rates apply to everybody, paying little mind to where they live or work.
## FAQ
### Does Social Security Count as Income?
Social Security benefits are not counted as gross income. However, benefits are remembered for your combined income, which the IRS uses to decide whether you ought to pay taxes on your benefits. Combined not entirely set in stone by totaling your [adjusted gross income (AGI)](/agi), nontaxable interest, and half of your Social Security benefits. In the event that your combined income is between $25,000 and $34,000, you might be taxed on up to half of your benefits. On the off chance that your combined income is more than $34,000, you might be taxed on up to 85% of your benefits.
### Which Country Has the Highest Federal Income Tax?
The Ivory Coast has the highest tax rate at 60%. Finland (56.95%), Japan (55.97), Denmark (55.9), Austria (55), Sweden (52.9%), Aruba (52%), Belgium (half), Israel (half), and Slovenia (half) round out the main 10.
### When Is Federal Income Tax Due?
Generally, federal income tax is due on April 15 of each and every year. The day can shift marginally if April 15 falls on a weekend or due to different factors.
### Which U.S. President Imposed the First Federal Income Tax?
President Abraham Lincoln was the president to impose the first federal income tax by signing the Revenue Act on Aug. 5, 1861. He did so because to finance the Civil War. A 3% tax was imposed on all annual incomes more than $800.
### What Are the Federal Income Tax Brackets for 2021 and 2022?
The U.S. federal income tax is a marginal tax rate system in view of an individual's income and filing status. For the 2021 and 2022 tax years, the tax brackets are 10%, 12%, 22%, 24%, 32%, 35%, and 37%.
