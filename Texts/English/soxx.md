# PHLX Semiconductor Sector Index (SOX)
## What Is the PHLX Semiconductor Sector Index (SOX)?

The PHLX Semiconductor Sector Index (SOX) is a [capitalization-weighted index](/capitalizationweightedindex) made out of 30 semiconductor companies. The companies in the index have primary business operations that include the design, distribution, production, and sale of semiconductors. The index is designed to follow the performance of listed semiconductors.

## Understanding the PHLX Semiconductor Sector Index (SOX)

The PHLX Semiconductor Sector Index (SOX) was made by the [Philadelphia Stock Exchange](/phlx) (PHLX) in 1993. It keeps on being managed by the Exchange with support from its owner [Nasdaq.](/nasdaq) Real-time data is provided daily from the PHLX.

To be remembered for the index, a [security](/security) must meet the accompanying criteria defined by the PHLX and Nasdaq.

- A stock must have business operations that principally center around the design, distribution, production, and sale of semiconductors.
- Stocks must be listed on the Nasdaq, [New York Stock Exchange](/nyse) (NYSE), NYSE American, or the CBOE Exchange.
- Shares can incorporate common stocks, ordinary shares, [American depositary receipts](/adr) (ADRs), shares of beneficial interest, or limited partnership interests.
- Securities must have a base [market capitalization](/marketcapitalization) of something like $100 million.
- Individuals must have traded somewhere around 1.5 million shares in every one of the six calendar months leading up to the reference date.
- The security has been trading for something like three months on a registered market, excluding the period of initial listing.
- The security must have listed options on a registered market in the U.S. or then again be eligible to do as such.
- The security can't be in that frame of mind of [bankruptcy](/bankruptcy) procedures.

## Investing in the PHLX Semiconductor Sector Index (SOX)

The SOX is a closely watched index for investors interested in technology chip stocks. [Options](/choice) on the index are actively traded. Investors interested in investing in the index's parts can investigate [exchange traded funds](/etf) (ETFs).

A famous ETF index fund that tracks the SOX is the Invesco PHLX Semiconductor ETF (SOXQ). Another is the VanEck Vectors Semiconductor ETF (SMH).

There are likewise [leveraged](/influence) and inverse leveraged ETFs tracking the index: the Direxion Daily Semiconductor Bull 3X Shares (SOXL) managed by Rafferty Asset Management, LLC and the Direxion Daily Semiconductor Bear 3X Shares (SOXS) managed by Rafferty Asset Management, LLC.

## The Philadelphia Stock Exchange (PHLX)

The PHLX is the most seasoned stock exchange in the United States. It was acquired by Nasdaq in 2008 and centers around the trading of options. Its listings incorporate equity options, index options, and foreign exchange options.

The SOX is one of several engaged sector indexes made by the PHLX. Different indexes incorporate the PHLX Housing Sector, PHLX Oil Service Sector, PHLX Utility Sector, and the PHLX Gold/Silver Sector Index.

## Features
- The SOX is a modified market capitalization-weighted index listed on the Philadelphia Stock Exchange in cooperation with Nasdaq Inc.
- Tech investors can shift focus over to several ETF products that track the SOX.
- The PHLX Semiconductor (SOX) is an equity index made out of companies principally engaged with the production and sale of semiconductor products like microchips, PCs, and networking equipment.
