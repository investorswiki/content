# Bank Confirmation Letter (BCL)
## What Is a Bank Confirmation Letter (BCL)?

A bank confirmation letter (BCL) is a letter from a bank or [financial institution](/financialinstitution) affirming the presence of a loan or a [line of credit](/lineofcredit) that has been extended to a borrower. The letter authoritatively vouches for the way that the borrower — typically an individual, company, or association — is eligible to borrow a predetermined amount of funds for a predefined purpose.

## How a Bank Confirmation Letter (BCL) Works

A bank confirmation letter's purpose is to guarantee an outsider, generally a seller, that the borrower approaches adequate financial resources to complete a transaction, like the purchase of goods. The confirmation letter — some of the time known as a [comfort letter](/comfort_letter) — isn't a guarantee of payment, yet just an assurance of the borrower's financial resources to make payment.

Bank confirmation letters typically require the signature of agents of the bank or the financial institution who are authorized to issue such correspondence.

Since a letter of confirmation is issued concerning a specific transaction or project, it's not transferable to an alternate transaction or project. On the off chance that the bank's customer chooses to go into an alternate deal or purchase, the customer as a rule is required to get another letter of confirmation.

For instance, a prospective home buyer chooses to buy an unexpected home in comparison to the one determined in the bank confirmation letter; another BCL would be required.

Regulations change from one country to another in terms of whether and how much a letter of confirmation must state the specific purpose for which a loan or credit extension is being extended to the borrower.

## Common Uses of a Bank Confirmation Letter

Bank confirmation letters are generally commonly prepared for a business customer of the bank, vouching for the presence of a predefined credit extension. The letters frequently console sellers of a large number of goods.

They may likewise be issued for a company that is going into a [joint venture project](/jointventure) with another company. While the letter doesn't guarantee payment or provision of funds, it gives an assurance of a high likelihood of the company getting payment from the bank's customer.

> A bank confirmation letter guarantees every interested individual in a business transaction that the bank's customer (the borrower) has, or has available, the fundamental financial resources to close the transaction.
>

The most common utilization of a bank confirmation letter by an individual is during the purchase of a home or land. In such cases, the letter gives confirmation to a seller or realtor that the bank's customer is approved for a [mortgage](/mortgage) up to a predetermined amount for a proposed purchase.

The letter isn't a commitment to buy the property; it is just a reassurance that the bank's customer approaches funds to complete a purchase. By and large, a prospective buyer can not close on a property without having a bank confirmation letter close by.

## Bank Confirmation Letter FAQs
### How Do I Get a Bank Confirmation Letter?

A bank confirmation letter can be received from your bank upon request. The bank will issue the letter with the fitting signatures and give it to you.

### What Is a Bank Certification Letter?

A bank certification letter is a letter issued by a bank that affirms an individual has an account with that bank and the total value of the funds in the account.

### What Is a Bank Verification Letter?

A bank verification letter is equivalent to a bank certification letter; a letter from a bank affirming that an individual has an account at that bank with the total value of the funds in the account.

### How Do I Get a Bank Confirmation Letter From My Bank?

To get a bank confirmation letter from your bank you might request face to face at a bank branch from one of the bankers, by a call to the bank, and contingent upon the financial institution, through their online platform.
