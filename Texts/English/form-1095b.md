# Form 1095-B
## What Is Form 1095-B: Health Coverage?

Form 1095-B is a [Internal Revenue Service (IRS)](/irs) form that may be shipped off taxpayers who receive [minimum essential health insurance coverage](/least essential-coverage) as defined by the [Affordable Care Act](/affordable-care-act) (ACA).

A 1095-B form shows information such as the health insurance coverage, effective coverage dates, individuals covered, and the coverage provider.

## Understanding Form 1095-B: Health Coverage

Form 1095-B: Health Coverage contains health care coverage information for taxpayers, their spouse, and their wards on the off chance that they were enrolled through an insurance provider or a self-protected employer.

Under the Affordable Care Act (ACA), there is a requirement for least essential coverage as part of the individual mandate for health insurance, also called the individual shared responsibility provision.

Most health insurance plans given by an employer will typically qualify as least essential coverage. Other qualifying plans include government-sponsored programs, as [Medicare](/medicare) and most [Medicaid](/medicaid) plans.

### Shared Responsibility Payment

Under the original rules of the ACA, taxpayers were required to have least essential health insurance coverage for each period of the year (or have a coverage exemption).

Prior to the 2020 plan year (for which you filed taxes in 2021), on the off chance that you or your family members didn't have either, you may have had to pay a penalty called a shared responsibility payment (also in some cases alluded to as the Individual Mandate penalty). Nonetheless, as part of the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (TCJA), the penalty for not having health insurance was eliminated. This also means that an exemption is not generally required.

In addition, beginning with the tax year 2019, the IRS acknowledged that the provider's obligation to supply 1095-B forms isn't an enforcement priority. Assuming providers comply with two conditions the IRS has set forward, they won't be penalized.

These are the two conditions: first, the provider's website contains a notice that Form 1095-B is available to participants upon request and includes specific information on the most proficient method to request it, and second, the provider must then outfit Form 1095-B in the span of 30 days of a request.

### State Penalties

Some U.S. states have executed their own penalties for the people who don't have health insurance coverage for the whole tax year. For example, Massachusetts charges half of the cost of the most minimal cost plan that could have been purchased. There are income-based exemptions to the penalty.

Taxpayers ought to check with their state and local governments to decide whether there is a penalty for not having least essential health insurance coverage all through the tax year.

## Form 1095-B versus Form 1095-A versus Form 1095-C

Health insurance providers send Form 1095-B to individuals for whom they give least essential coverage. Form 1095-B gives information about who was covered and when. You will receive [Form 1095-A: Health Insurance Marketplace Statement](/form-1095a) in the event that you enrolled in a qualified health plan via the health insurance marketplace.

In the event that you received or were offered healthcare from a 50 or more employer employees, you may receive [1095-C: Employer-Provided Health Insurance Offer and Coverage](/form-1095c) in addition to, or instead of, a 1095-B form.

## Instructions to File Form 1095-B: Health Coverage

Contingent upon who gave your health insurance coverage, as just noted, you ought to receive either Form 1095-A, Form 1095-B, or Form 1095-C. You usually have to give information from these forms or acknowledge receiving one of them on your federal tax return. You don't have to submit the actual form. Taxpayers tick off a box on their returns, indicating how long they were covered all through the tax year.
