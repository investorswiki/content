# Forward Rate
## What Is a Forward Rate?

A forward rate is an interest rate applicable to a financial transaction that will happen from here on out. Forward rates are calculated from the spot rate and are adjusted for the cost of carry to determine the future interest rate that compares the total return of a more extended term investment with a strategy of rolling over a more limited term investment.

The term may likewise allude to the rate fixed for a future financial obligation, for example, the interest rate on a loan payment.

## Grasping Forward Rates

In [forex](/forex), the forward rate determined in an agreement is a contractual obligation that must be regarded by the gatherings in question. For instance, consider an American exporter with a large export order pending for Europe, and the exporter embraces to sell 10 million euros in exchange for dollars at a forward rate of 1.35 euros per U.S. dollar in six months' time. The exporter is committed to deliver 10 million euros at the predefined forward rate on the predetermined date, no matter what the situation with the export order or the [exchange rate](/exchangerate) winning in the spot market around then.

Thus, forward rates are widely utilized for the purpose of hedging in the currency markets, since [currency forwards](/currencyforward) can be tailored for specific requirements, dissimilar to [futures](/futures), which have fixed contract sizes and expiry dates and accordingly can't be altered.

With regards to bonds, forward rates are calculated to determine [future values](/futurevalue). For instance, an investor can purchase a one-year [Treasury bill](/treasurybill) or buy a six-month bill and roll it into an additional half year bill once it develops. The investor will be apathetic assuming that the two investments produce a similar total return.

For instance, the investor will know the [spot rate](/spot_rate) for the half year bill and will likewise know the rate of a one-year bond at the inception of the investment, however they won't have the foggiest idea about the value of a six-month bill that will be purchased six months from now.
