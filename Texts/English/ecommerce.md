# Electronic Commerce (Ecommerce)
## What Is Electronic Commerce (Ecommerce)?

The term electronic commerce (ecommerce) alludes to a [business model](/businessmodel) that permits companies and individuals to buy and sell goods and services over the Internet. Ecommerce works in four major market sections and can be conducted over computers, tablets, smartphones, and other smart gadgets. Practically every possible product and service is accessible through ecommerce transactions, including books, music, boarding passes, and financial services like stock investing and online banking. In that capacity, it is considered a very [disruptive technology](/disruptive-technology).

## Understanding Ecommerce

As verified above, ecommerce is the most common way of buying and selling unmistakable products and services online. It includes more than one party alongside the exchange of data or [currency](/currency) to deal with a transaction. Part of the greater industry is known as electronic business (ebusiness), which includes each of the processes required to run a company online.

Ecommerce has helped businesses (especially those with a narrow arrive at like small businesses) gain access to and lay out a more extensive market presence by giving less expensive and more efficient distribution channels for their products or services. Target (TGT) enhanced its [brick-and-mortar](/brickandmortar) presence with an online store that permits customers to purchase all that from garments and coffeemakers to toothpaste and action figures right from their homes.

Ecommerce works in every one of the four of the accompanying major [market segments](/market-section). These are:

- [Business to business](/btob) (B2B), which is the direct sale of goods and services between businesses
- Business to consumer (B2C), which includes sales among businesses and their customers
- Consumer to consumer, which permits individuals to sell to each other, ordinarily through a third-party site like eBay
- Consumer to business, which lets individuals sell to businesses, for example, an artist selling or licensing their craftsmanship for use by a corporation

Giving goods and services isn't quite as simple as it might appear. It requires a ton of research about the products and services you wish to sell, the market, crowd, competition, as well true to form business costs.

Whenever that is determined, you want to concoct a name and set up a legal structure, like a corporation. Next, set up an ecommerce site with a [payment gateway](/payment-gateway). For example, a small business owner who runs a dress shop can set up a website advancing their dress and other related products online and permit customers to make payments with a credit card or through a payment processing service, like [PayPal](/paypal).

The [industry](/industry) has gone through such countless changes from that point forward, bringing about a great deal of development. New technology keeps on making it simpler for individuals to do their online shopping. ## Benefits and Disadvantages of Ecommerce

Web based business offers consumers the accompanying benefits:

- **Convenience**: E-commerce can happen 24 hours per day, seven days per week.
- **Increased selection**: Many stores offer a more extensive exhibit of products online than they carry in their brick-and-mortar counterparts. And many stores that exclusively exist online may offer consumers exclusive inventory that is inaccessible somewhere else.

In any case, there are certain downsides that accompany ecommerce sites, too. The drawbacks include:

- **Restricted customer service**: If you shop online for a computer, you can't just ask an employee to show a particular model's elements in person. And albeit a few websites let you talk online with a staff member, this is definitely not a commonplace practice.
- **Absence of instant gratification**: When you buy a thing online, you must sit tight for it to be sent to your home or office. Notwithstanding, [e-tailers](/electronic-retailing-e-following) like Amazon make the waiting game somewhat less difficult by offering same-day delivery as a premium option for select products.
- **Powerlessness to touch products**: Online pictures don't be guaranteed to convey the whole story about a thing, and so web based business purchases can be sub-par when the products received don't match consumer expectations. Case in point: a thing of apparel might be produced using shoddier fabric than its online picture demonstrates.
