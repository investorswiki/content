# Graduate Management Admission Test (GMAT)
## What Is the Graduate Management Admission Test (GMAT)?

The GMAT, which represents the graduate management admission test, is a state sanctioned test expected to measure a test taker's aptitude in mathematics, verbal skills, and scientific composition. The GMAT is most commonly utilized as the primary exam investigated by business schools to gain entrance into a MBA program. The exam is generally offered by computer just; in areas of the world where computer networks are limited, the exam might be given as a paper-based test.

## Grasping the GMAT

The GMAT exam comprises of four sections: scientific composing assessment, verbal thinking, integrated thinking, and quantitative thinking. The maximum score attainable for the GMAT is 800, and exam scores are generally legitimate for a very long time following the exam's completion. On average, the exam requires three and one-half hours to complete.

## How the Graduate Management Admission Test Is Applied

The Graduate Management Admission Council controls the exam. As well as testing understanding of composing and math, the GMAT is additionally used to evaluate a person's critical thinking skills and logic as applicable to business and management in reality. Starting in 2012, the exam added a section called Integrated Reasoning, which surveys a singular's evaluation skills for dealing with data assembled from numerous sources and in new configurations. This section likewise plans to test students with regards to working with data and technology.

In excess of 2,100 graduate programs and institutions around the world utilize the GMAT to evaluate candidates to their programs. The Graduate Management Admission Council has suggested that the GMAT be utilized as one factor among others for deciding if a student is accepted into a program. Moreover, the council alerts that for a few international students, the composing analysis section could show the limits of their understanding of the English language, instead of their critical reasoning and thinking capacity.

It isn't uncommon for graduate programs to utilize either the GMAT or the [Graduate Record Examination](/gre) (GRE) to survey a candidate. Due to the differences in the manner the two tests are scaled, GMAT and GRE scores can't be straightforwardly compared.

Besides, the idea of the exams and what they test the candidates on make it unseemly to likewise treat the two exams. The Graduate Management Admission Council suggests not utilizing a supposed cutoff score while inspecting candidates, yet rather to check their comprehensive application out. Assuming a cutoff score is carried out, the council proposes the institution go to extra lengths to show that the cutoff doesn't lead to discrimination in light of age, sex, or nationality.
