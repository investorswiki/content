# Volume Price Trend Indicator (VPT)
## What Is a Volume Price Trend Indicator (VPT)?

The volume price trend (VPT) indicator decides a security's price direction and strength of price change. The indicator consists of a cumulative volume line that adds or deducts a numerous of the [percentage change](/percentage-change) in a share price's trend and current volume, contingent on the security's vertical or descending developments.

## Understanding the Volume Price Trend Indicator (VPT)

The volume price trend indicator is utilized to decide the balance between a security's demand and supply. The percentage change in the share price [trend](/trend) shows the relative supply or demand of a specific security, while volume demonstrates the force behind the trend. The VPT indicator is like the [on-balance volume (OBV) indicator](/onbalancevolume) in that it measures cumulative volume and gives traders information about a security's money flow. Most charting software bundles have the VPT indicator included.

## Trading With the Volume Price Trend Indicator

**Signal Line Crossovers:** A [signal line](/signal_line), which is just a moving average of the indicator, can be applied and used to create trading signals. For instance, a trader might buy a stock when the VPT line crosses over its signal line and sell when the VPT line passes below its signal line.

**Confirmations:** The VPT indicator can be utilized in conjunction with moving averages and the [average directional index (ADX)](/adx) to confirm trending markets. For example, a trader could buy a stock on the off chance that the 20-day moving average is over the 50-day moving average and joined by rising VPT indicator values. Conversely, the trader might choose to sell assuming the 20-day moving average is below the 50-day [moving average](/movingaverage) and the indicator's values are falling.

The ADX likewise measures trend and [momentum](/momentum) and can be utilized with the VPT indicator to confirm that a market is trending. ADX readings over 25 demonstrate that a security is trending, while readings below 25 show sideways price action. In this way, a [trader](/trader) could buy when the ADX is over 25 and the VPT line is over its signal line. They could sell when the ADX has a value below 25 and the VPT line is below its signal line.

**Divergence:** Traders can utilize the VPT indicator to spot technical divergence. Divergence happens when the indicator makes a higher high or a lower low, however the security's price makes a lower high or a higher low. Traders ought to place a [stop-loss order](/stop-lossorder) over the latest swing high or below the latest swing low to limit risk.
