# Stock Quote
## What Is a Stock Quote?

A stock quote is the price of a stock as quoted on an exchange. A fundamental quote for a specific stock gives data, like its bid and ask price, last traded price, and volume traded.

## Grasping Stock Quotes

All [stocks](/stock) in the U.S. have been quoted in decimals, as opposed to divisions, since April 9, 2001. Subsequently, [bid-ask spreads](/bid-askspread) have contracted emphatically, with spreads for the most widely traded stocks now as small as a penny, compared with 1/sixteenth of a dollar (or $0.0625) prior. Decimal pricing has brought about substantial savings on [transaction costs](/transactioncosts) to U.S. investors in light of more tight bid-ask spreads.

Investors progressively access stock [quotes](/quote) online or on mobile gadgets, for example, cell phones, instead of through print media, like papers and magazines. A large number of Internet entrances and sites offer delayed stock quotes at no charge, with real-time stock quotes generally restricted to paying supporters.

Stock quotes can be given supplemental data and data, for example, the high and low prices for given security that have been recorded throughout the trading day. It might likewise show the change in the value of the security compared against the prior day's [closing price](/closingprice) or the opening price of the current trading day. This difference in price may be displayed as a percentage, uncovering how much security has increased or declined in value. Analyst proposals for a given security could likewise be given a stock quote. Such suggestions could be displayed for hourly, daily, week by week, and month to month spans.

Think about the stock quote for the social media behemoth Meta (formerly Facebook). It is shown alongside supplemental data: the company's ticker symbol (META), change in price (communicated in terms of percentage), and last quoted price at closing time.

Contingent upon the service and platform giving stock quotes, the data could rigorously spin around the current, most recent pricing, or there can be expanded subtleties like metrics on the daily, week after week, month to month, and annual performance of the security. A stock quote can likewise remember performance metrics for share prices for a long term period.

The pricing showed with a stock quote mirrors the buying and selling activity that impacts the value of a given security. As each trading day unfurls, news and industry trends connected with a security can influence the manner in which investors decide to handle the shares. At the point when beneficial updates are revealed, for example, strong revenue and earnings reported by the company or positive test results for a product, the value of the shares can increase as additional investors buy into the company. These [shifts](/perspective change) are reflected in the stock quotes that shareholders and different watchers of the company will use to pursue speculation choices.
