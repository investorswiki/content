# Haas School of Business
## What Is the Haas School of Business?

The Haas School of Business is the business school of the University of California at Berkeley. Founded in 1898 and situated in Berkeley, California, the school offers both undergraduate and graduate programs.

The Haas School of Business has the differentiation of being the most established American business school to be founded at a public university. It is additionally known for its high quality of guidance; it is habitually positioned as among the main 10 best business schools in the world.

## Understanding the Haas School of Business

Recently known as the University of California's College of Commerce, the Haas School of Business received its current name in 1989, following an almost $25 million gift by the businessman and [philanthropist](/magnanimity), Walter A. Haas Jr. Today, Haas School of Business is home to around 2,500 understudies spread across their undergraduate and graduate programs, as well as north of 240 employees.

The Haas School of Business' programs incorporates undergraduate majors in core business school subjects, for example, [finance](/finance), [accounting](/financialaccounting), and [marketing](/marketing); as well as specific undergraduate programs in [entrepreneurship](/business visionary), international business, and, surprisingly, the crossing point of business and science. ## Illustration of the Haas School of Business

For 2021, the Haas School of Business' full-time MBA program was rated as the seventh best program in the United States by US News and The Economist, and it was rated as the nation's eighth (twelfth all around the world) by the Financial Times.

With annual tuition of generally $60,000, Haas School of Business MBA graduates saw a median starting salary of roughly $140,000 in 2020, of which just under 90% received their proposals in the span of 90 days of graduation. In recent years, these graduates have found employment chiefly in the [technology](/technology_sector), management counseling, and financial services sectors.
