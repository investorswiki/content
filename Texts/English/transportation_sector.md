# Transportation Sector
## What Is the Transportation Sector?

The transportation sector is a category of companies that offer types of assistance to move individuals or goods, as well as transportation infrastructure. Technically, transportation is a sub-gathering of the industrials sector as per the [Global Industry Classification Standard (GICS).](/gics) The transportation sector comprises of several industries including air freight and logistics, airlines, marine, road and rail, and transportation infrastructure. These industries are additionally broken down into the sub-industries air freight and logistics, airlines, marine, railroads, shipping, airport services, highways and rail tracks, and marine ports and services.

## Figuring out the Transportation Sector

The performance of companies in the transportation industry is highly sensitive to variances in company earnings and the price of transportation services. Fundamental factors influencing company earnings incorporate fuel costs, labor costs, demand for services, international occasions, and government regulation. Large numbers of these factors are interconnected. For instance, if the U.S. government passes regulations that make it more hard for individuals to earn their commercial drivers' license, this will reduce the supply of drivers, driving up the cost of hiring drivers.

Oil prices are a key factor for transportation, as the product's price generally impacts transportation expenses. Gas and fuel prices that rise will increase costs for a shipping company, eating into their profit and possibly decreasing their stock price.

Energy costs and the value of transportation stocks are absolutely interrelated. Low energy costs might turn into a factor in helping the share price of different transportation companies, however the influence can likewise be switched. At the point when demand for transportation services is high the impact will be reflected in quarterly reports of transportation companies. This data may, once scattered, may persuade energy traders to bid up prices for oil and comparative commodities. Notwithstanding, in the event that demand for commercial transportation falls, this data could lead to a decline in oil prices too.

## Investing in the Transportation Sector

You can invest in companies that move individuals and products by buying shares of individual transportation companies, or through sector-explicit mutual funds or exchange-traded funds (ETFs) that have practical experience in the transportation sector. The transportation sector is one of the most broadly diversified with industrial companies addressing airlines, railroads, drivers, equipment and leasing stocks, and logistics companies. Funds that track this sector will follow a benchmark sector index like the DJTA.

### The Dow Jones Transportation Index

The [Dow Jones Transportation Average](/djta) (DJTA) is a price-weighted average of 20 transportation stocks traded in the United States. The DJTA is, truth be told, the most established U.S. stock index, first assembled in 1884 by Charles Dow, fellow benefactor of Dow Jones and Company. The index initially comprised of nine railroad companies, a testament to their dominance of the U.S. transportation sector in the late nineteenth and mid twentieth hundreds of years, and two non-railroad companies. Notwithstanding railroads, the index currently incorporates airlines, shipping, marine transportation, delivery services and logistics companies.
