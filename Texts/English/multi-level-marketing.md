# Multilevel Marketing (MLM)
## What Is Multilevel Marketing (MLM)?

The term marketing (MLM) alludes to a strategy utilized by some direct [sales](/sale) companies to sell products and services. MLM urges existing individuals to advance and sell their offerings to others and carry on newcomers into the business. Wholesalers are paid a percentage of their recruits' sales. Newcomers become the wholesaler's [network](/networking) or downline and are, thus, urged to make sales to earn money. Numerous MLM schemes are legal yet there are illegal operations that are run as [pyramid schemes](/pyramidscheme).

## Grasping Multilevel Marketing (MLM)

Multilevel marketing is a genuine business strategy that is regularly utilized by businesses that depend (intensely) on sales to create [revenue](/revenue). Dissimilar to traditional sales channels, multilevel marketing programs include the utilization of networks for sales and to select new participants. Thusly, they're frequently alluded to as network marketing.

This is the secret. People are brought into the business as contractors, independent business owners, wholesalers, or direct salespeople. These individuals are then entrusted with selling the company's products or potentially services to other people, including family and friends. Sales should be possible in person or online. They are given a [commission](/commission) for each sale they make.

Participants are likewise urged to carry in or select others into the program as participants. While they may not be constrained to do as such, signing up new contractors gives a financial incentive to participants, who receive a percentage of the sales of their recruits. what's more, their recruits, and of their recruits, etc.

There can be hundreds — even thousands — of participants, contingent upon the size of a company. Individuals at all levels receive some form of commission, as long as the chain continues onward. The more layers there are, the more money individuals can make. Think of it as a pyramid. The person or individuals at the top earn the most while the people who sit toward the base earn less commission dollars. Moderately few, however, generally earn any significant [income](/income) from their efforts.

> Since multilevel marketing plans are commission-based, the participants don't receive salaries.
>
## Special Considerations

In spite of the fact that it is legal, multilevel marketing is frequently disputable. One problem is pyramid schemes that utilization money from newcomers to pay individuals at the top as opposed to the people who perform the work. These schemes (and individuals behind them) exploit others by professing to be taken part in real multilevel or [network marketing](/network-marketing). You can spot pyramid schemes by their greater spotlight on enlistment than on product sales.

An issue in determining the authenticity of a multilevel marketing company is whether it sells its products principally to consumers or to its individuals who must select new individuals to buy their products. On the off chance that it is the former, the company is probable a real multilevel advertiser. In the event that it is the last option, it very well may be an illegal pyramid scheme.

Moderately few earn significant incomes from their efforts. To certain onlookers, this mirrors the qualities of a pyramid scheme. That is the reason the [Federal Trade Commission](/ftc) (FTC) has been researching multilevel marketing companies for a very long time.

> You can frequently spot pyramid schemes by their greater spotlight on enrollment than on product sales.
>
## Genuine Examples of Multilevel Marketing

There are a ton of instances of multilevel marketing in the corporate world. Coming up next are just two of those most famous and notable companies that operate in this circle.

### Amway

Amway is a notable direct sales company that utilizes MLM to produce revenue. The company, which sells wellbeing, excellence, and home care products in excess of 100 countries, reported $8.8 billion in sales conducted by its independent business owners in 2018.

### Herbalife Nutrition

Herbalife Nutrition is a high-profile MLM company that makes and disperses weight-misfortune and dietary products. The company contends that a large portion of its revenue is from product sales — not enlistment. It likewise says it offers individuals numerous protections, like a money-back guarantee, so they won't be left with products they couldn't sell.

There have been different lawsuits against Herbalife blaming it for distorting its sales works on, incorporating a settlement came to with the FTC in 2016, under which it needed to [restructure](/rebuilding) its business.

Activist investor William Ackman likewise shed a national spotlight on the company by shorting $1 billion of the company's stock in 2012. Ackman blamed the company for operating a pyramid scheme, backing up his charges with a bet that the stock price would fall under the weight of the scam. He abandoned that bet in 2018. As of Sept. 10, 2021, the company's stock price traded at $53.67 a share.

## How Do you Define Multilevel Marketing?

By and large, marketing is a sales structure where individuals from a company are urged to enlist new individuals. When enrolled, this salesperson receives a cut of their spotter's sales. Simultaneously, every salesperson profits from the sale of a given product.

## Is Multilevel Marketing a Pyramid Scheme?

Multilevel marketing is questionable and frequently compared to pyramid schemes. While some multilevel marketing operations are legal, others have gone under investigation. This commonly happens when the majority of the activity's profits funnel up to the top, leaving little until the end of its individuals.

At the point when an organization centers principally around enlistment, as opposed to selling products, this may likewise signal that it is operating under a pyramid scheme. Once in a while, individuals from these schemes will number in the hundreds or even thousands.

## What Is an Example of Multilevel Marketing?

Avon is an illustration of multilevel marketing. The company operates under a model where sales are driven through a network of salespeople, through introductions or one-on-one settings in homes or businesses. Like a number of other multilevel marketing businesses, Avon commonly doesn't operate a fixed retail location. The parent company, all things being equal, gives the apparatuses and resources to entrepreneurs to conduct their business at different locations. This type of business model is likewise alluded to as a direct sales model.
