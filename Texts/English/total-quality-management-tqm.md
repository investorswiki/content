# Total Quality Management (TQM)
## What Is Total Quality Management (TQM)?

Total quality management (TQM) is the continual course of detecting and reducing or eliminating errors in manufacturing, streamlining [supply chain management](/scm), improving the customer experience, and ensuring that employees depend on speed with training. Total [quality management](/quality-management) means to hold all parties involved in the production cycle accountable for the overall quality of the final product or service.

TQM was developed by William Deming, a management consultant whose work incredibly affected Japanese manufacturing. While TQM shares a lot of in common with the Six Sigma improvement process, it isn't equivalent to [Six Sigma](/six-sigma). TQM centers around ensuring that internal guidelines and handle standards reduce errors, while Six Sigma hopes to reduce absconds.

## Understanding Total Quality Management (TQM)

Total quality management is a structured approach to overall organizational management. The focal point of the cycle is to work on the quality of an organization's outputs, including goods and services, through the continual improvement of internal practices. The standards set as part of the TQM approach can reflect both internal needs and any industry standards right now in place.

Industry standards can be defined at different levels and may include adherence to different laws and regulations governing the operation of a particular business. Industry standards can likewise include the production of things to a grasped standard, even on the off chance that the standard isn't backed by official regulations.

## Primary Principles of Total Quality Management

TQM is viewed as a customer-zeroed in process that spotlights on reliably improving business operations. It endeavors to guarantee all associated employees pursue the common objectives of improving product or service quality, as well as improving the procedures that are in place for production.

> Special accentuation is put on reality based decision making, using performance metrics to monitor progress; high levels of organizational communication are empowered to maintain employee involvement and confidence.
>
## Industries Using Total Quality Management

While TQM originated in the manufacturing sector, its principles can be applied to various industries. With an emphasis on long-term change as opposed to short-term objectives, it gives a strong vision to systemic change. In light of this, TQM is utilized in numerous industries, including, yet not limited to, manufacturing, banking and finance, and medicine.

These methods can be applied to all departments within an individual organization also. This guarantees all employees are working toward the objectives set forward for the company, improving function in every area. Involved departments can include administration, marketing, production, and employee training.

## FAQ
### What Is an Example of TQM in Practice?
Maybe the most renowned illustration of TQM is Toyota's implementation of the [Kanban](/kanban) system. A **kanban** is a physical signal that makes a chain reaction, resulting in a specific action. Toyota utilized this plan to execute its just-in-time (JIT) inventory process.To make its assembly line more efficient, the company chose to keep just sufficient inventory close by to fill customer orders as they were created. Thusly, all parts of Toyota's assembly line are assigned a physical card that has an associated inventory number. Right before a part is installed in a vehicle, the card is taken out and climbed the supply chain, effectively requesting one more of a similar part. This permits the company to keep its inventory lean and not overload superfluous assets. Effective quality management brought about better vehicles that could be created at an affordable price.
### How Does Total Quality Management Respond?
TQM directs movements of every sort and tasks expected to maintain an ideal level of greatness within a business and its operations. This includes the determination of a quality policy, creating and implementing quality planning and assurance, and quality control and quality improvement measures.
### What Are the Principles of TQM?
Different cycles of TQM have been developed, each with its own set of principles. All things considered, certain core components endure. These include, among others: Good initiative; an emphasis on quality; putting the customer first; mistake remedy and improvement as an on-going cycle; and job training.
