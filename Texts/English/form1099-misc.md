# Form 1099-MISC
## What Is Form 1099-MISC: Miscellaneous Income?

Form 1099-MISC: Miscellaneous Income (or Miscellaneous Information, as it's currently called) is a [Internal Revenue Service (IRS)](/irs) form used to report certain types of miscellaneous compensation, like rents, prizes and awards, healthcare payments, and payments to an attorney.

Before the 2020 tax year, Form 1099-MISC was additionally used to report non-representative compensation for independent contractors, freelancers, [sole proprietors](/soleproprietorship), and self-employed individuals. Starting with 2020, this non-worker pay is reported on Form 1099-NEC: Nonemployee Compensation.These forms generally report business payments — not personal ones.

A 1099-MISC form is one of numerous in the 1099 series and among those normally utilized. Taxpayers receive 1099s, including Form 1099-MISC, shortly after the finish of the tax year.

## Who Files Form 1099-MISC: Miscellaneous Income?

Form 1099-MISC: Miscellaneous Income (also known as Miscellaneous Information) is completed and conveyed by somebody who has paid something like $10 in royalties or broker payments in lieu of dividends or tax-exempt interest to another person. It's additionally shipped off every person to whom you paid somewhere around $600 during the calendar year in the accompanying categories:

- Rents (real estate agents and property managers report rent paid to property owners, for example, or you report the office space rent you paid)
- Prizes and awards
- Other income payments
- Medical and healthcare payments (made in the course of your trade or business)
- Crop insurance proceeds
- Cash payments for fish (or other aquatic life) purchased from any individual who earns enough to pay the rent getting fish
- Cash paid from a [notional principal](/notionalprincipalamount) contract to an individual, partnership, or estate
- Payments to an attorney
- Any fishing boat proceeds

The form is likewise used to report direct sales of no less than $5,000 of consumer products to a buyer for resale anywhere other than a permanent retail establishment.

The payer must send the form to the recipient by February 1 and file it with the IRS by March 1 (March 31 if filing electronically). The recipient can connect the form to their tax return.

> The IRS overhauled Form 1099-MISC in 2020 and introduced another form, called Form 1099-NEC, for non-representative compensation (which previously was reported in Box 7 of Form 1099-MISC).
>
## Instructions to File Form 1099-MISC: Miscellaneous Income

A multipart fillable Form 1099-MISC is available on the IRS website.

Copy An of Form 1099-MISC appears in red. This copy of the form isn't intended for printing; it is for IRS utilize as it were.

The black parts of the form can be completed, downloaded, and printed:

- Copy 1 goes to the recipient's state tax department
- Copy B is shipped off the recipient
- Copy 2 is shipped off the recipient for their state tax return
- Copy C is retained by the payer for record-keeping

The payer incorporates their name, address, and tax identification number, as well as the recipient's name, address, and [Social Security Number](/ssn).

The revised 1099-MISC form has different box numbers for reporting various types of payments — for example, Rents in Box 1 and Royalties in Box 2. On the off chance that applicable, you'll likewise finish up Box 4: Federal Income Tax Withheld and Box 16: State Tax Withheld.

## Features
- Form 1099-MISC is utilized to report miscellaneous compensation like rents, prizes and awards, medical and healthcare payments, and payments to an attorney.
- Non-representative compensation is currently reported on Form 1099-NEC.
- A taxpayer would receive a Form 1099-MISC on the off chance that you paid them $10 or more in royalties or $600 or more in other types of miscellaneous income during a calendar year.
- Until 2020, it additionally was utilized to report the income of taxpayers who are not employees, like independent contractors, freelancers, sole-proprietors, and self-employed individuals.
