# Zig Zag Indicator
## What Is the Zig Zag Indicator?

The Zig Zag indicator lowers the impact of random price variances and is utilized to assist with recognizing price [trends](/trend) and changes in price trends.

## Grasping the Zig Zag Indicator

The Zig Zag indicator plots points on a chart at whatever point prices reverse by a percentage greater than a pre-picked variable. Straight lines are then drawn, interfacing these points.
<!--F3494985914F4E3A6A5CCF506F516A1E-->
The indicator is utilized to assist with distinguishing price trends. It takes out random price variances and endeavors to show trend changes. Zig Zag lines possibly seem when there is a price movement between a [swing high](/swinghigh) and a swing low that is greater than a predetermined percentage — frequently 5%. By sifting minor price movements, the indicator makes trends simpler to recognize in all time spans.

The Zig Zag indicator is in many cases utilized related to Elliot Wave Theory to decide the positioning of each wave in the overall cycle. Traders can explore different avenues regarding different percentage settings to see what gives the best outcomes. For instance, a setting of 4% may characterize waves more obviously than a setting of 5%. Stocks have their own examples, so almost certainly, traders should advance the Zig Zag indicator's percentage setting to suit those [securities](/security).

Albeit the Zig Zag indicator doesn't anticipate future trends, it assists with recognizing likely support and resistance zones between plotted swing highs and swing lows. Zig Zag lines can likewise uncover reversal designs, for example [double bottoms](/doublebottom) and head and shoulders tops. Traders can utilize well known technical indicators, for example, the relative strength index (RSI) and the stochastics oscillator to affirm whether the price of a security is overbought or oversold when the Zig Zag line changes heading.

> A [momentum investor](/momentum_investing) could utilize the indicator to remain in a trade until the Zig Zag line affirms the other way. For instance, on the off chance that the investor holds a [long position](/long), they wouldn't sell until the Zig Zag line rotates toward the ground.

### Instructions to Calculate the Zig Zag Indicator
1. Pick a starting point (swing high or swing low).
1. Pick % price movement.
1. Distinguish next swing high or swing low that varies from the starting point = > % price movement.
1. Draw [trendline](/trendline) from starting point to new point.
1. Recognize next swing high or swing low that contrasts from the new point = > % price movement.
1. Draw trendline.
1. Repeat to latest swing high or swing low.

## Zig Zag Indicator Limitations

Like other trend-following indicators, buy and [sell signals](/sell-signal) depend on past price history that may not be predictive of future price action. For instance, the majority of a trend might have previously happened when a Zig Zag line at last shows up.

Traders ought to know the latest Zig Zag line may not be permanent. At the point when price changes course, the indicator begins to draw another line. On the off chance that that line doesn't arrive at the indicator's percentage setting and the price of the security reverses heading, the line is taken out and supplanted by an extended Zig Zag line in the trend's original course.

Given the lag, numerous traders utilize the Zig Zag indicator to affirm the course of the trend instead of endeavoring to time a perfect entry or exit.
